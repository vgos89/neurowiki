## 2. Population

Symptomatic intracranial atherosclerotic disease, high-recurrence subset:

- **Qualifying event:** TIA or non-disabling ischemic stroke within **30 days** before enrollment
- **Stenosis:** angiographically verified **70–99%** of the diameter of a major intracranial artery (mean ~80% in both arms; ~10–12% had 90–99% stenosis)
- **Geography:** 50 US sites (all-US enrollment — limits generalizability to non-US populations, especially Asian ICAD cohorts where CASSISS later tested similar question)
- **Age:** mean 59.5 ± 11.8 (medical), 61.0 ± 10.7 (PTAS) — adults; no upper age stated as exclusion
- **Race:** ~71% White, ~22–25% Black
- **Qualifying artery:** ICA 20–22%, MCA 41–46%, vertebral 10–17%, basilar 22%
- **Qualifying event type:** ~63–67% stroke, ~33–37% TIA
- **Antithrombotic at time of qualifying event:** 62–65% (i.e., these were "on-treatment failures")
- **Imaging selection:** angiographic confirmation of stenosis only; **no perfusion or hemodynamic-failure selection** (this is the key generalizability constraint vs. modern selected-stenting populations)
- **NIHSS:** non-disabling stroke required; specific NIHSS range not specified as inclusion (severity excluded only via "non-disabling" qualifier)

**Generalizability to NeuroWiki audience:** Direct relevance to neurology residents managing symptomatic ICAD ≥70% within 30 days of qualifying event. Does **not** generalize to asymptomatic ICAD, ICAD with hemodynamic failure on perfusion imaging, or to populations selected by modern criteria (compare WEAVE for on-label, experienced-operator subset).

## 4. Intervention and comparator

**Both arms received aggressive medical management (AMM). The PTAS arm received PTAS in addition.**

- **AMM (both arms, verbatim from Methods):**
  - Aspirin 325 mg/day
  - Clopidogrel 75 mg/day **for 90 days after enrollment**
  - Primary risk factor targets: SBP **<140 mm Hg** (<130 mm Hg if diabetes), LDL **<70 mg/dL** (1.81 mmol/L)
  - Rosuvastatin (Crestor) provided to study
  - Lifestyle modification program (INTERVENT)
- **PTAS arm (additional):** Percutaneous transluminal angioplasty and stenting with the **Gateway PTA Balloon Catheter + Wingspan self-expanding nitinol stent system** (Boston Scientific / Stryker Neurovascular). Procedure within 3 business days of randomization, under general anesthesia. Clopidogrel 600 mg loading dose 6–24 h before PTAS if not already on 75 mg/day for ≥5 days. Operators credentialed on the basis of ≥20 most-recent prior intracranial stenting/angioplasty cases.

**Note:** This is the **Wingspan self-expanding stent**, not the Vitesse balloon-expandable stent (VISSIT — separate trial; see §8).

## 5. Primary endpoint (verbatim from Methods, page 995)

> "The primary end point is stroke or death within 30 days after enrollment or after a revascularization procedure for the qualifying lesion during the follow-up period (i.e., angioplasty for symptomatic restenosis in a patient in the PTAS group or placement of a stent in a patient in the medical-management group) or ischemic stroke in the territory of the qualifying artery between day 31 and the end of the follow-up period."

**Time horizon:** composite over the entire follow-up period (designed for mean 2 years); the principal between-arm contrast is the **30-day event rate** plus the **1-year cumulative probability**. The trial design used a two-sided log-rank test with Kaplan–Meier estimation; reported probabilities are KM-derived.

**Important framing for NeuroWiki:** the endpoint is **not** mRS shift, not mRS 0–2 dichotomization, not all-cause stroke — it is a composite of (a) periprocedural stroke/death within 30 days, (b) any same-territory ischemic stroke beyond day 30. This is an **event-rate composite**, which is why the display archetype is harm-framed and **not** Grotta-bar.

---

## 6. Statistical framework

**Primary design type:** `superiority` (frequentist, intent-to-treat, two-sided log-rank, α=0.05).

- **Powered to detect:** 35% relative reduction in primary endpoint with PTAS, assuming 24.7% projected 2-year rate in AMM (adjusted from 29% WASID baseline assuming 15% relative AMM benefit), at 80% power, with 5% crossover and 2% loss-to-follow-up assumptions.
- **Target n:** 764 (382 per arm).
- **Actual n at stop:** 451 randomized (227 AMM, 224 PTAS). Enrollment **halted by DSMB on April 5, 2011** for safety (excess periprocedural stroke in PTAS) plus futility (interim analysis indicated virtually no chance of demonstrating PTAS benefit).
- **Stopping characterization:** mixed — *harm in the PTAS arm* (DSMB cited safety) plus *futility for PTAS benefit*. There were no pre-specified safety stopping rules; the harm signal was qualitatively adjudicated by the DSMB.
- **Interim analyses:** two pre-planned efficacy interims at ~33% and ~66% of expected primary events. P-values reported as two-sided, **not adjusted for multiple testing** (per Methods).

**This is not:** noninferiority, Bayesian, ordinal-shift, registry, dose-finding, workflow, or imaging-selection.

## 7. Primary result

**30-day primary endpoint (stroke or death within 30 days):**

| Arm | n with event / N | KM probability at 30 d (95% CI) | Composition |
|---|---|---|---|
| PTAS + AMM | 46 / 224 (20.5%) | **14.7%** (10.7–20.1) | nonfatal stroke 12.5%, fatal stroke 2.2% |
| AMM alone | 26 / 227 (11.5%) | **5.8%** (3.4–9.7) | nonfatal stroke 5.3%, non-stroke-related death 0.4% |

- **Between-arm comparison at 30 days:** P = **0.002** (z-test on the 30-day cumulative probability)
- **Absolute risk difference at 30 days:** **+8.9 percentage points** in favor of AMM (i.e., harm of PTAS)
- **NNH (30 days) ≈ 11** for stroke/death from adding PTAS (derived; not reported in paper). NeuroWiki should display this as harm framing only, not as a benefit NNT.

**1-year cumulative probability of primary endpoint:**

| Arm | KM probability at 1 yr (95% CI) |
|---|---|
| PTAS + AMM | **20.0%** (15.2–26.0) |
| AMM alone | **12.2%** (8.4–17.6) |

- **Overall log-rank P-value (entire follow-up at time of report):** **P = 0.009**
- **As-treated analysis:** same conclusion, P = 0.009
- **Beyond 30 days, same-territory ischemic stroke:** **13 events in each arm** — i.e., the entire between-arm difference is driven by **periprocedural (early) events**, not by late protection from stenting.

**Display archetype:** **`bar-binary`** (Archetype A) — binary primary, superiority RCT, but the inference is **harm in the intervention arm**. The trial-listing should label this as a harm-framed superiority trial; do **not** swap to `grotta-bar` (no ordinal outcome) or `risk-table-km` (the KM curve exists but the primary inference is the 30-day binary contrast).

## 8. Key safety results

| Safety endpoint | PTAS arm | AMM arm | Comment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage ≤30 d | **10 / 224 (4.5%)** | **0 / 227 (0%)** | P = 0.04 (Fisher exact). 4 fatal. Of these 10: 5 peri-procedural parenchymal (1 guidewire perforation, 1 thalamic at reperfusion), 4 subarachnoid, 1 unclear mechanism. |
| Non-stroke-related death ≤30 d | 1 (0.4%) | 1 (0.4%) | Similar |
| Stroke-related death ≤30 d | 5 (2.2%) | 0 | |
| Death (any cause) full follow-up | 7 (3.1%) | 7 (3.1%) | KM 1-yr: 4.1% (PTAS) vs 3.4% (AMM), P = 0.95 |
| Any stroke (full follow-up) | 50 (22.3%) | 32 (14.1%) | P = 0.03 |
| Disabling or fatal stroke (full follow-up) | 19 (8.5%) | 13 (5.7%) | P = 0.21 |
| Major hemorrhage (any) | 22 (9.8%) | 5 (2.2%) | P < 0.001 |
| Myocardial infarction | 5 (2.2%) | 7 (3.1%) | P = 0.60 |

**Driver of harm:** **periprocedural stroke**, predominantly within the first 1–6 days post-PTAS. Of 33 strokes in the PTAS group within 30 days, **25 occurred within 1 day** of the procedure and 8 within days 2–6. The risk did not decrease over the enrollment period (P = 0.20 for trend) and did not differ by site enrollment volume (13.5% at top-enrolling vs 14.7% at others, P = 0.77), arguing against a pure operator-experience explanation.

**Mechanism note for NeuroWiki:** the 4.5% symptomatic ICH in the PTAS arm is the most clinically actionable harm signal — neurology residents should associate Wingspan ICH risk with this number when explaining why on-label, post-AMM-failure stenting is reserved.

## 9. Expert and editorial caveats

### Accompanying editorial / contemporaneous commentary
- No co-published editorial in the same NEJM issue. Editorial commentary appeared in subsequent issues and in other journals; the principal contemporary critique was Abou-Chebl A, Steinmetz H. *Stroke* 2012 — argued operator-experience and patient-selection limitations. SAMMPRIS investigators responded in Stroke 2012 (PMC3748816) defending credentialing thresholds.

### Subsequent literature (chain context for trial-page)
- **VISSIT (Zaidat OO et al., JAMA 2015;313:1240–1248; DOI 10.1001/jama.2015.1693):** independent confirmation of harm — different (balloon-expandable Vitesse) stent, same direction. 30-day primary safety composite **24.1% (stent) vs 9.4% (medical)**; 30-day intracranial hemorrhage **8.6% vs 0%**. Stopped early after SAMMPRIS results. Reinforces that the harm signal is not Wingspan-specific.
- **WEAVE (Alexander MJ et al., Stroke 2019;50:889–894; DOI 10.1161/STROKEAHA.118.023996):** FDA-mandated post-market surveillance, **single-arm registry** (Archetype G in NeuroWiki). N=152 on-label patients (per FDA Wingspan label: 22–80 yo, ≥2 strokes on medical therapy, 70–99% ICAS, ≥7 days post-most-recent stroke, mRS ≤3). 72-hour stroke/death rate **2.6%** — met the pre-specified **<4%** safety benchmark. **Does NOT overturn SAMMPRIS**; addresses a narrower, post-AMM-failure population with experienced operators.
- **CASSISS (Gao P et al., JAMA 2022;328(6):534–542; DOI 10.1001/jama.2022.12000):** Chinese RCT, n=380, symptomatic ICAS 70–99%, stenting vs medical at 1 year — **no significant difference** (8.0% stent vs 7.2% medical at 30 d; 9.9% vs 9.0% at 1 yr). Confirms no benefit of routine stenting in symptomatic ICAS even in a population with lower medical-arm event rates.
- **Meta-analyses (e.g., Tsivgoulis et al. J Neurol 2022; multiple Cochrane reviews):** consistently conclude stenting is not superior and is associated with peri-procedural harm in unselected symptomatic ICAD.

### Guideline incorporation
- **AHA/ASA 2021 Guideline for the Prevention of Stroke in Patients With Stroke and TIA** (Kleindorfer et al., Stroke 2021;52:e364–e467; DOI 10.1161/STR.0000000000000375; PMID 34024117):
  - **Class III: No Benefit / Harm**, LOE B-R, for **Wingspan stent system as initial treatment** in symptomatic 70–99% intracranial stenosis, even for on-antithrombotic patients at time of index event.
  - Aggressive medical therapy (DAPT 90 days, high-intensity statin, BP <140/90, lifestyle) is **Class I**.
  - AHA/ASA 2026 currency note: per stroke-guidelines skill, the 2021 guideline remains the current AHA/ASA secondary-prevention reference. NeuroWiki citation IDs should reflect this version until 2021 is superseded.
- **AAN 2022 Practice Advisory** (Turan TN et al., Neurology 2022;98(12):486–498): same conclusion — aggressive medical therapy preferred; stenting considered only for highly selected refractory patients at experienced centers.

### Required NeuroWiki-side caveats (must appear in `legend` or `howToInterpret`)
- "Stopped early for harm — DSMB halted enrollment at 451/764 planned due to PTAS safety + futility."
- "Population: 70–99% ICAS within 30 days of qualifying TIA/stroke; results do not generalize to asymptomatic ICAS or to hemodynamic-failure-selected populations."
- "Does not establish that all intracranial stenting is unsafe — establishes that **Wingspan stenting is not superior to AMM and carries periprocedural harm in this population**. See WEAVE for the on-label, post-medical-failure registry context."
- "Periprocedural symptomatic ICH 4.5% vs 0% is the dominant harm driver."

### `doesNotProve` (explicit limits)
- Does **not** prove all intracranial stenting is harmful — specific to Wingspan, symptomatic 70–99%, within-30-day qualifier.
- Does **not** apply to asymptomatic ICAD.
- Does **not** address salvage stenting after documented AMM failure (no RCT addresses this; WEAVE registry is the closest evidence).
- Does **not** speak to angioplasty alone (small subset; underpowered).
- Does **not** establish that AMM by itself is causally responsible for the low 5.8% rate — concurrent improvements in stroke care during 2008–2011 may contribute.
