## Citation

- **Author:** Mas J-L, Derumeaux G, Guillon B, et al. for the CLOSE Investigators
- **Title:** Patent Foramen Ovale Closure or Anticoagulation vs. Antiplatelets after Stroke
- **Journal:** N Engl J Med 2017;377(11):1011–1021
- **DOI:** 10.1056/NEJMoa1705915
- **PMID:** 28902593
- **ClinicalTrials.gov:** NCT00562289
- **Published:** September 14, 2017

## Trial identity

- Investigator-initiated, multicenter, open-label, three-randomization-group superiority trial. Blinded endpoint adjudication.
- 32 sites in France + 2 sites in Germany.
- Enrollment December 2007 to December 2016.
- Funded by French Ministry of Health.

## Population

- Patients 16–60 years old, recent ischemic stroke (within 6 months) attributed to PFO with **associated atrial septal aneurysm OR large interatrial shunt** (>30 microbubbles within 3 cardiac cycles).
- ASA defined as septum primum excursion >10 mm on TEE.
- Workup ruled out large-artery disease, cardioembolic source, small-vessel/lacunar disease, hypercoagulable state, dissection.
- Prolonged (≥30-day) ECG monitoring not required.
- **Critical:** restricted to high-risk PFO morphology (ASA OR large shunt). This is the narrowest population of the three 2017 trials.

## Randomization

- 1:1:1 (PFO closure + long-term antiplatelet | antiplatelets alone | oral anticoagulation).
- Patients with contraindication to anticoagulation → randomized only between PFO closure and antiplatelet.
- Patients with contraindication to PFO closure → randomized only between anticoagulation and antiplatelet.

## Primary outcome and result

- **Primary efficacy outcome:** occurrence of fatal or nonfatal stroke.
- **PFO closure vs antiplatelet (randomization groups 1 + 2; N = 238 vs 235):**
  - Stroke 0/238 (0%) vs 14/235 (6.0%)
  - **HR 0.03 (95% CI 0.00–0.26), P < 0.001**
  - 5-year Kaplan–Meier estimate 4.9 percentage points lower with PFO closure
  - Authors-reported NNT to prevent 1 stroke over 5 years: **20 (95% CI 17–25)**
- Mean follow-up: 5.3 ± 2.0 years.
- **Anticoagulant vs antiplatelet (randomization groups 1 + 3; N = 187 vs 174):** 3 vs 7 strokes; HR 0.44 (0.11–1.85), underpowered, **statistical significance not analyzed**.

## Safety — atrial fibrillation signal

- Atrial fibrillation or flutter: **11/238 (4.6%) PFO closure vs 2/235 (0.9%) antiplatelet, P = 0.02**.
- 10 of 11 PFO-closure AF cases occurred within 30 days of procedure; AF did not recur during a median 4.4-year follow-up. Transient procedure-associated AF, not durable arrhythmic substrate.
- Procedural complications 14/238 (5.9%): atrial fibrillation/flutter (9 + 1), supraventricular tachycardia (2), air embolism (1), hyperthermia (1).
- Major bleeding: 0.8% PFO closure vs 2.1% antiplatelet (NS).
- No procedure-related death. One death in PFO closure (pancreatic cancer, unrelated).

## Statistical design

- **primaryDesign: binary-superiority** (time-to-event stroke, log-rank).
- **primaryResult: met** (PFO closure vs antiplatelet, P < 0.001).
- Authors-reported NNT is interpretable here — binary superiority over a time-to-event endpoint with a single dichotomization.
- Two one-sided superiority hypotheses with Bonferroni adjustment (alpha 0.035 per hypothesis).
- Enrollment stopped early by sponsor (December 18, 2014) due to budget; follow-up continued. **Stopped-early-administrative**, not for efficacy.

## §8 editorial context

### §8a Accompanying NEJM editorial
- Ropper AH. Tipping Point for Patent Foramen Ovale Closure. N Engl J Med 2017;377(11):1093–1095. DOI 10.1056/NEJMe1709637.
- Central interpretation: the 2017 cluster (CLOSE, RESPECT long-term, REDUCE) represents a tipping point. Ropper argued that the path forward is to define **PFO morphological characteristics that make paradoxical embolism likely** (large shunt, atrial septal aneurysm) rather than relying purely on the exclusion-defined "cryptogenic" label. He cautioned that restricting closure to high-risk features alone may be too conservative but that the boundary is becoming clearer.

### §8b Post-publication NEJM letters
- Letters in NEJM 2017 December issue raised methodological concerns shared across the three trials: (1) variable definitions of "high-risk" PFO features across CLOSE (required ASA or large shunt), RESPECT (no morphology requirement), and REDUCE (broader shunt grades); (2) different devices used (Amplatzer in RESPECT; multiple devices in CLOSE; Helex/Cardioform in REDUCE) limit pooling; (3) detection of post-procedure atrial fibrillation depended on clinical surveillance rather than prolonged monitoring — true incidence may be underestimated.

### §8c Subsequent guideline incorporation
- **AHA/ASA 2021 Secondary Prevention Guideline** (Kleindorfer DO et al., Stroke 2021;52:e364–e467, DOI 10.1161/STR.0000000000000375): In non-lacunar ischemic stroke at age <60 with a PFO and no other apparent stroke etiology, PFO closure is reasonable (Class IIa, Level B-R). The guideline cites CLOSE, RESPECT, and REDUCE as the supporting evidence and emphasizes shared decision-making about the atrial fibrillation trade-off.
- **AAN 2020 Practice Advisory Update** (Messé SR et al., Neurology 2020;94:876–885): in patients <60 with PFO and embolic-appearing cryptogenic stroke, clinicians may recommend closure following discussion of potential benefits and risks.
- **ESO 2019 Guideline** (Pristipino C et al., Eur Stroke J 2019): supports PFO closure in selected patients 18–60 years.

### §8d Subsequent meta-analyses
- Mir H, Siemieniuk RAC, Ge L, et al. BMJ 2018;362:k2515 — IPD meta-analysis of all 6 RCTs (CLOSURE I, PC, RESPECT, CLOSE, REDUCE, DEFENSE-PFO): pooled HR for recurrent stroke 0.36 (95% CI 0.17–0.79) with PFO closure vs medical therapy, NNT ~37 over 5 years. AF risk increased (OR 4.7).
- Kent DM, Saver JL, Kasner SE, et al. **PASCAL classification system** (JAMA 2021;326:2277–2286): IPD pooling stratified patients into "unlikely," "possible," and "probable" PFO-attributable strokes by RoPE score + PFO morphology. PFO closure benefit concentrated in "probable" group (RoPE ≥7 with high-risk PFO features).
- The 2018 BMJ pooled analysis and the 2021 PASCAL classification confirm what CLOSE alone established: closure works best in young patients with high-risk PFO morphology and no competing stroke etiology — exactly the CLOSE inclusion criteria.

## Verbatim quoted text (for registry)

> "Among patients who had had a recent cryptogenic stroke attributed to PFO with an associated atrial septal aneurysm or large interatrial shunt, the rate of stroke recurrence was lower among those assigned to PFO closure combined with antiplatelet therapy than among those assigned to antiplatelet therapy alone. PFO closure was associated with an increased risk of atrial fibrillation."

(NEJM 2017;377:1011, Conclusions paragraph, abstract p.1011)
