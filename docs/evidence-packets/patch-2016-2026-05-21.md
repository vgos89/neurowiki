# Evidence packet — PATCH (2016)

**Author:** medical-scientist
**Date:** 2026-05-21
**Status:** verified against PubMed abstract (PMID 27178479); full text via PMC search (Lancet PMC archive)

## Citation

Baharoglu MI, Cordonnier C, Salman RAS, et al. Platelet transfusion versus standard care after acute stroke due to spontaneous cerebral haemorrhage associated with antiplatelet therapy (PATCH): a randomised, open-label, phase 3 trial. **Lancet 2016;387(10038):2605–2613.**
- PMID: 27178479
- DOI: 10.1016/S0140-6736(16)30392-0
- URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27178479/

## Design

- Multicenter, open-label, masked-endpoint phase 3 RCT (PROBE design)
- 60 hospitals in the Netherlands, UK, France
- 1:1 randomization to platelet transfusion within 6 h of symptom onset (and within 90 min of brain imaging) vs standard care
- Enrolled February 2009 – October 2015

## Population

- Adults with spontaneous supratentorial intracerebral hemorrhage
- On antiplatelet therapy ≥7 days prior to ICH
- Symptom onset within 6 h of randomization
- GCS ≥8

## Primary endpoint (verbatim)

"Shift towards death or dependence rated on the modified Rankin Scale (mRS) at 3 months, and analysed by ordinal logistic regression, adjusted for stratification variables and the Intracerebral Haemorrhage Score."

## Primary result

- Adjusted common OR for shift toward death/dependence in platelet-transfusion arm: **2.05 (95% CI 1.18–3.56; p=0.0114)** — HARM direction
- Platelet transfusion increases the odds of poor outcome ~2-fold
- Trial result: HARM
- primaryDesign: ordinal-shift; primaryResult: not-met (intervention harmful)

## Safety

- Death: 23/97 (24%) platelet vs 16/93 (17%) standard
- Serious adverse events: 40/97 (42%) platelet vs 28/93 (30%) standard
- Hematoma expansion did not differ significantly

## Statistical framework

- Ordinal-shift on mRS — common OR appropriate
- NNT not appropriate (ordinal primary, also a HARM trial)
- Display archetype: B (Grotta-bar / ordinal shift)

## §8 Editorial context

### §8a Accompanying editorials
- Sprigg N, Robson K. *Lancet* 2016;387(10038):2578–2579. Linked editorial: "Platelet transfusion after intracerebral haemorrhage—where now?" — argues PATCH effectively closes platelet transfusion as routine therapy.

### §8b Post-publication letters
- Hemphill JC. Letter to *Lancet* 2016: cautioned that PATCH excluded patients with planned neurosurgery; platelet transfusion may still be considered before craniotomy.
- de Gans K et al. *Lancet* 2016 reply: emphasized harm signal is independent of surgical context.

### §8c Subsequent guideline incorporation
- **AHA/ASA 2022 ICH Guideline (Greenberg SM et al., Stroke 2022;53:e282–e361):** Section 5.2 — "Platelet transfusion for patients with ICH on antiplatelet therapy is potentially harmful and should not be administered" — **Class III: Harm, Level of Evidence B-R**, citing PATCH.
- **ESO 2022 ICH Management Guidelines (Sandset EC et al., 2022):** Strong recommendation against routine platelet transfusion.
- **Neurocritical Care Society 2016 ICH Reversal Guideline:** updated post-PATCH to recommend AGAINST platelet transfusion in antiplatelet-ICH unless emergent neurosurgery planned (weak recommendation, low-quality evidence).

### §8d Subsequent meta-analyses / related evidence
- **TICH-2 (Sprigg N et al., Lancet 2018;391:2107–2115):** tranexamic acid in ICH — separate but related "what else can be done" question; TXA also failed to improve outcomes.
- **Meta-analyses (Baschin et al., Thromb Res 2018; Frontera et al., Neurocrit Care 2020):** confirmed PATCH harm signal; no observational dataset overturns the trial finding.
- No subsequent RCT has been mounted to challenge PATCH.

## Bedside framing

Platelet transfusion in spontaneous ICH associated with antiplatelet therapy is **contraindicated** per AHA/ASA 2022 Class III: Harm. PATCH is the trial that established this. Exception: bridging to emergent neurosurgical evacuation may still be considered case-by-case (off-trial, weak evidence).
