## §1 Trial identity and design

CREST-2 comprised **two parallel, multicenter, observer-blinded RCTs** at 155 sites in Australia, Canada, Israel, Spain, and the United States. Each trial randomized patients with asymptomatic, high-grade (≥70%) extracranial carotid stenosis to revascularization plus intensive medical management vs intensive medical management alone:

- **Stenting trial:** intensive medical management alone (medical-therapy group, n=629) vs carotid-artery stenting plus intensive medical management (stenting group, n=616). Total N=1245.
- **Endarterectomy trial:** intensive medical management alone (medical-therapy group, n=623) vs carotid endarterectomy plus intensive medical management (endarterectomy group, n=617). Total N=1240.

**Each trial has its own primary endpoint analysis and its own conclusion.** They share an identical intensive medical-management protocol and identical primary-outcome definition.

**Primary outcome (both trials):** 4-year composite of any stroke (ischemic or hemorrhagic) or death assessed from randomization to day 44 (periprocedural period), OR ipsilateral ischemic stroke assessed during the remaining follow-up period up to 4 years (postprocedural period). Analyzed by intention-to-treat.

**Eligibility:**
- Age ≥35 years
- No history of stroke, TIA, or amaurosis fugax in the carotid-artery territory within 180 days before randomization
- ≥70% stenosis by Doppler ultrasonography (peak systolic velocity ≥230 cm/s plus one of: end-diastolic velocity ≥100 cm/s, peak systolic velocity ratio of internal-to-common carotid ≥4.0, or ≥70% on CTA/MRA); or ≥70% on catheter angiography alone

**Exclusions:** previous disabling stroke, unstable angina, atrial fibrillation prompting anticoagulation.

**Intensive medical management protocol (identical across all 4 arms):**
- Target SBP <130 mm Hg (originally <140; lowered in 2018 after guideline changes)
- Target LDL cholesterol <70 mg/dL (1.80 mmol/L); alirocumab available no cost after 2018 (donated by Regeneron)
- Management of glucose, glycated hemoglobin, smoking, body weight, physical inactivity
- Telephone health coaching
- Protocol-driven central oversight

**Operator credentialing:** all interventionists and surgeons were certified via a validated process. Interventionists submitted preceding 12 months of CAS cases plus 3-25 prospective nonurgent procedural reports/angiograms; surgeons submitted preceding 50 consecutive cases with periprocedural stroke/death rate <3%.

**Statistical analysis:** Treatment differences assessed at 4 years using Kaplan-Meier with cumulative-incidence function for competing risk of death. Sample-size calculation assumed a 4-year event rate of 3.6% in the medical-therapy group with 85% power to detect rates <0.8% or >8.4% in the medical-therapy arms (accounting for 5% crossover each direction and 2.5% annual withdrawal). Pre-specified interim analysis adjusted final alpha to 0.047. 95% CIs reported are 95.3% intervals adjusted to reflect the bootstrap distribution thresholds.

## §2 Primary results — by trial

### Stenting trial (CAS vs IMM)

- **4-year primary composite:** 6.0% (95% CI 3.8 to 8.3) in medical-therapy group vs **2.8% (95% CI 1.5 to 4.3)** in stenting group
- **Absolute risk difference:** 3.2 percentage points (95% CI 0.6 to 5.9); **P=0.02** for the absolute difference
- **Relative risk (medical-therapy vs stenting):** 2.13 (95% CI 1.15 to 4.39)
- **Periprocedural component (days 0-44):**
  - Medical-therapy: 0 of 629 events (0.0%, 95% CI 0.0 to 0.6)
  - Stenting: 8 of 616 events (1.3%, 95% CI 0.6 to 2.5); 7 strokes and 1 death
- **Postprocedural ipsilateral ischemic stroke (>44 days):**
  - Medical-therapy: 28 events in 600 patients followed for 1686 person-years; annual rate 1.7% (95% CI 1.1 to 2.4)
  - Stenting: 7 events in 582 patients followed for 1714 person-years; annual rate 0.4% (95% CI 0.2 to 0.9)
  - Relative risk (medical-therapy vs stenting): 4.07 (95% CI 1.78 to 9.31)
- **Tipping-point analysis:** significance retained unless 4+ events removed from medical-therapy group OR 3+ events added to stenting group
- **NNT (per published Discussion):** 31 to prevent one primary-outcome event over 4 years

### Endarterectomy trial (CEA vs IMM)

- **4-year primary composite:** 5.3% (95% CI 3.3 to 7.4) in medical-therapy group vs **3.7% (95% CI 2.1 to 5.5)** in endarterectomy group
- **Absolute risk difference:** 1.6 percentage points (95% CI −1.1 to 4.3); **P=0.24** for the absolute difference
- **Relative risk (medical-therapy vs endarterectomy):** 1.43 (95% CI 0.78 to 2.72)
- **Periprocedural component (days 0-44):**
  - Medical-therapy: 3 of 623 events (0.5%, 95% CI 0.1 to 1.4)
  - Endarterectomy: 9 of 617 events (1.5%, 95% CI 0.7 to 2.8)
- **Postprocedural ipsilateral ischemic stroke (>44 days):**
  - Medical-therapy: 23 events in 600 patients followed for 1761 person-years; annual rate 1.3% (95% CI 0.9 to 2.0)
  - Endarterectomy: 10 events in 596 patients followed for 1823 person-years; annual rate 0.5% (95% CI 0.3 to 1.0)
  - Relative risk: 2.38 (95% CI 1.13 to 5.00)
- **Tipping-point analysis:** significance would have been achieved if 7+ events removed from endarterectomy group OR 6+ events added to medical-therapy group

### Combined periprocedural + postprocedural annual event rates

- Stenting trial — medical-therapy alone: 1.6%/y (95% CI 1.1 to 2.3); 28 events / 1759 person-years
- Endarterectomy trial — medical-therapy alone: 1.4%/y (95% CI 1.0 to 2.1); 26 events / 1834 person-years

## §3 Verbatim quotations from publication

The following are verbatim quotations from Brott et al., NEJM 2026;394(3):219-231 (PDF read 2026-05-20):

- *Conclusions (Abstract, p.219):* "Among patients with high-grade stenosis without recent symptoms, the addition of stenting led to a lower risk of a composite of perioperative stroke or death or ipsilateral stroke within 4 years than intensive medical management alone. Carotid endarterectomy did not lead to a significant benefit."

- *Discussion lead (p.225):* "Among participants with asymptomatic high-grade carotid stenosis, the stenting trial showed that the addition of transfemoral carotid-artery stenting to intensive medical management led to a significantly lower risk of the primary composite outcome (periprocedural stroke or death or postprocedural ipsilateral stroke within 4 years) as compared with intensive medical management alone (2.8% vs. 6.0%). The absolute risk difference of 3.2 percentage points was significant, corresponding to a number needed to treat of 31 to prevent one primary-outcome event."

- *Endarterectomy primary (p.225):* "In the endarterectomy trial, we found no significant difference in the 4-year incidence of primary-outcome events (5.3% in the medical-therapy group and 3.7% in the endarterectomy group)."

- *Limitation — operator generalizability (p.228):* "revascularization was performed only by well-trained and certified high-volume operators, so the results in CREST-2 may not reflect practice more broadly."

- *Limitation — modern medical-therapy drift (p.227):* "various changes in medical-therapy practices occurred during the trials that could lower stroke rates and negate any additional benefit of revascularization."

- *Closing statement (p.228):* "We found that among patients with high-grade stenosis without recent stroke symptoms, carotid-artery stenting with intensive medical management led to a lower risk of a composite outcome of perioperative stroke or death or ipsilateral stroke within 4 years than intensive medical management alone. Carotid endarterectomy plus intensive medical management did not provide a significant benefit as compared with intensive medical management alone."

## §4 Statistical design — taxonomy classification

Per the `trial-statistics` skill:

- **Statistical framework:** binary-superiority for each trial. Each trial tested the addition of revascularization against intensive medical management with a binary 4-year composite primary outcome (any stroke or death by day 44, or ipsilateral ischemic stroke thereafter). Frequentist hypothesis test with adjusted alpha 0.047 after the pre-specified interim analysis.
- **Stenting trial primary result:** met (P=0.02; ARD 3.2 pp favoring stenting). NNT 31 (reported by authors).
- **Endarterectomy trial primary result:** not-met (P=0.24; ARD 1.6 pp, 95% CI crosses zero). NNT not appropriate to display for a not-met endpoint.
- **Display:** binary-superiority allows NNT for the stenting arm. ARD with CI is reported; relative risks given alongside.

## §5 Safety

Periprocedural and postprocedural serious adverse events through 4 years (NEJM Table S5, summarized in text p.225):

**Stenting trial:**
- Carotid revascularization (re-do or for symptom progression): 118 (18.8%) medical-therapy vs 29 (4.7%) stenting
- All-cause death by 4 years: 69 (11.0%) medical-therapy vs 48 (7.8%) stenting

**Endarterectomy trial:**
- Carotid revascularization: 131 (21.0%) medical-therapy vs 44 (7.1%) endarterectomy
- All-cause death by 4 years: 60 (9.6%) medical-therapy vs 54 (8.8%) endarterectomy

Periprocedural strokes: 0 of 629 (medical, stenting trial); 7 strokes + 1 death in 616 (CAS, 1.3%); 3 of 623 (medical, endarterectomy trial); 9 of 617 (CEA, 1.5%). Common reasons for revascularization in the medical-therapy groups: new carotid symptoms, progression of stenosis, patient preference.

## §6 Population

Demographics (NEJM Table 1):
- Mean age ~70 years (69.7 stenting trial; 70.5 endarterectomy trial)
- 35-39% female across arms
- ~90% White, ~6% Black, ~4-5% Hispanic ethnicity
- Hypertension 85-88%, dyslipidemia 90-93%, diabetes 34-41%, current smoking 19-21%
- Mean baseline SBP 138-139 mm Hg; baseline LDL 77-81 mg/dL
- mRS 0 (no symptoms) at baseline: 87-89%
- ≥70% stenosis in index artery: 97% across arms
- ≥50% stenosis in nonindex artery: 34-37%

Crossover during follow-up:
- Stenting trial medical-therapy group: 106 (17%) eventually underwent revascularization (78 stenting, 28 endarterectomy); 41 (7%) assigned to stenting received no procedure.
- Endarterectomy trial medical-therapy group: 111 (18%) eventually underwent revascularization (94 CEA, 17 CAS); 24 (4%) assigned to endarterectomy received no procedure (22 received stenting).

Risk-factor target achievement was generally sustained across arms — Figure 1 shows similar proportions in target SBP and LDL ranges between treatment groups within each trial. Average SBP and LDL targets were met by ~60-70% of patients by month 4 and beyond.

Follow-up duration: median 3.6 years (IQR 1.6 to 4.0) in stenting trial; median 4.0 years (IQR 2.0 to 4.0) in endarterectomy trial. First randomization December 9-10, 2014; last visits completed July 31, 2025 (stenting) and September 30, 2024 (endarterectomy).

## §7 Subgroups

Pre-specified subgroup analyses (Figure 3, NEJM): age (<70, ≥70, <80, ≥80), sex, peak systolic velocity (<342 vs ≥342 cm/s), hypertension, diabetes, dyslipidemia, smoking, CHA₂DS₂-VASc (0-3 vs ≥4), symptomatic status of target artery (lifetime asymptomatic vs previous stroke/TIA), symptomatic status of contralateral artery. CIs not adjusted for multiplicity; treated as hypothesis-generating per NEJM Discussion. The Discussion states: "Results appeared to be consistent across subgroups in the two trials."

### §8c Subsequent guideline incorporation

The relevant guideline context at the time of CREST-2 publication:
- **AHA/ASA/AAPA/ABC/ACPM/AGS/APhA/ASH/ASPC/NMA/PCNA 2017 Hypertension Guideline** (Whelton PK et al., Hypertension 2018;71:1269-1324) — SBP <130 mm Hg target that drove the 2018 CREST-2 protocol amendment.

### §8d Lineage and contradicting evidence

- **ACAS (1995)** — JAMA 1995;273:1421-1428. Asymptomatic ≥60% stenosis, CEA vs medical (aspirin only era). 5-year ipsilateral stroke 5.1% CEA vs 11.0% medical; ARR 5.9 pp. Established CEA for asymptomatic stenosis under 1990s medical management.
- **ACST (2004, 10-year follow-up 2010)** — Lancet 2004;363:1491-1502; Lancet 2010;376:1074-1084. Asymptomatic ≥60% stenosis (Europe). 5-year stroke 6.4% CEA vs 11.8% deferred CEA. ACST-2 (Lancet 2021) showed CEA and CAS gave similar 5-year results in asymptomatic stenosis.
- **SPACE-2 (2020)** — Reiff T et al., Stroke 2020;51:2960-2971. Asymptomatic carotid stenosis; underpowered (final cohort <16% of target). CREST-2 Discussion (p.227): SPACE-2 "did not show a benefit of revascularization as compared with medical therapy" but used less-stringent ECST stenosis criteria; limited power.
- **ECST-2 (2024)** — Donners SJA et al., Lancet Neurol 2024;23:389-399. 50-69% stenosis with one third symptomatic; only 10 of 214 revascularization-arm patients underwent CAS. 2-year interim results.
- **Oxford Vascular Study** — population-based observational data showed lower annual stroke rates than CREST-2 medical-therapy arms but relied on 50%+ stenosis seeking medical attention for event ascertainment. CT-based infarct detection (less sensitive than MRI used in CREST-2 for 82% of cases).
- **CREST (2010, mixed symptomatic + asymptomatic; CAS vs CEA)** — Brott TG et al., NEJM 2010;363:11-23 (DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa0912321). Established CAS as comparable to CEA across pooled symptomatic and asymptomatic populations; periprocedural stroke higher with CAS, MI higher with CEA.
- **ACT-1 (2016)** — CAS vs CEA NI in asymptomatic stenosis. NI established.

CREST-2 closes the question CREST 2010 / ACT-1 / ACST-2 could not address: how revascularization compares to **modern** intensive medical management (PCSK9 inhibitors, lower SBP target, statin adherence, antithrombotic optimization) in asymptomatic patients without recent symptoms.
