# Evidence Packet — PRoFESS (antiplatelet arm)

**Verifier:** evidence-verifier (clinical-trial-audit, trial-statistics skills)

## 1. Canonical citation

- **Title:** "Aspirin and Extended-Release Dipyridamole versus Clopidogrel for Recurrent Stroke"
- **First author:** Sacco RL, for the PRoFESS Study Group
- **Journal:** New England Journal of Medicine
- **Volume/Issue/Pages:** 359(12):1238–1251
- **Year:** 2008
- **DOI:** 10.1056/NEJMoa0805002 (resolves)
- **PMID:** 18753638
- **PMCID:** PMC2714259 (open-access confirmed)
- **NCT:** NCT00153062 (2×2 factorial registration)
- **Companion (telmisartan arm, do NOT duplicate-entry):** Yusuf S et al. NEJM 2008;359(12):1225–1237. DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa0804593. PMID 18753639.

## 2. Population

- **n:** 20,332 (ASA–ERDP 10,181; clopidogrel 10,151)
- **Mean age:** 66.1
- **Geography:** 695 centers across 35 countries
- **Qualifying event:** ischemic stroke within <90 days before randomization (>24h symptoms, or shorter with imaging evidence). TIA NOT a qualifying event.
- **Median time event→randomization:** 15 days (39.8% within 10 days)
- **Enrollment:** Sep 11, 2003 – Feb 8, 2008
- **Mean follow-up:** 2.5 years (range 1.5–4.4)
- **Key exclusions:** contraindications to study antiplatelets; planned other antiplatelet/anticoagulant; severe disability precluding follow-up.

**Generalizability flag:** completed ischemic stroke only — do not extrapolate to TIA/minor-stroke (CHANCE/POINT) acute window.

## 3. Intervention and comparator (verbatim)

- **ASA–ERDP arm:** "25 mg of aspirin plus 200 mg of extended-release dipyridamole twice daily" (Aggrenox formulation; ASA 50 mg/day + ER-DP 400 mg/day)
- **Clopidogrel arm:** "75 mg of clopidogrel daily"
- Double-dummy; orthogonal randomization to telmisartan vs placebo in 2×2 factorial.

## 4. Primary endpoint (verbatim)

> **"The primary outcome was first recurrence of stroke of any type."**

Time-to-event analyzed by Cox proportional hazards over mean 2.5-year follow-up.

## 5. Statistical framework

**Primary design type:** `noninferiority`

- **Null hypothesis:** ASA–ERDP inferior to clopidogrel (HR ≥ 1.075)
- **Pre-specified NI margin (Δ):** hazard ratio **1.075**
- **Direction:** one-sided α = 0.025 for NI; sequential two-sided α = 0.05 for superiority if NI met
- **Power:** 82% to detect 6.5% RRR, predicated on 1,715 recurrent strokes
- **Display archetype:** `ni-margin-chart`
- **NNT:** **NOT allowed** — noninferiority design, NI not established.

## 6. Primary result

| Arm | Events / n | Rate |
|---|---|---|
| ASA–ERDP | 916 / 10,181 | 9.0% |
| Clopidogrel | 898 / 10,151 | 8.8% |

- **HR (ASA–ERDP vs clopidogrel):** **1.01 (95% CI 0.92–1.11)**
- **NI established?** **NO.** Upper CI bound (1.11) crosses NI margin (1.075). Authors: "the upper limit of the confidence interval extends beyond the prespecified noninferiority margin of 1.075."
- **Caveat for copy:** Do NOT state "equivalent" or "as good as" — the trial formally **failed noninferiority**, despite overlapping event curves.

## 7. Key safety results (Table 3)

| Outcome | ASA–ERDP | Clopidogrel | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intracranial hemorrhage | 147 (1.4%) | 103 (1.0%) | **HR 1.42 (1.11–1.83)** |
| Major hemorrhagic events | 419 (4.1%) | 365 (3.6%) | HR 1.15 (1.00–1.32) |
| Death any cause | 739 (7.3%) | 756 (7.4%) | comparable |
| New/worsening CHF | 144 (1.4%) | 182 (1.8%) | HR 0.78 (0.62–0.96; P=0.02) |
| Drug discontinuation (any cause) | 29.1% | 22.6% | P<0.001 |

**Net safety:** ASA–ERDP shows statistically significant excess ICH (~+0.4% absolute) and higher discontinuation; clopidogrel showed unexplained higher new/worsening CHF.

## 8. Editorial caveats

- NEJM accompanying editorial text not retrieved this session — flag for medical-scientist follow-up before finalizing legend/caveat copy.
- NI margin critique: 7.5% relative-hazard margin is generous; NI not met even under this generous margin strengthens the inferential weight.
- ITT vs per-protocol: ~6.5 pp discontinuation differential raises an ITT/per-protocol concern; the primary analysis is ITT.
- AHA/ASA 2021 Secondary Prevention Guideline (Kleindorfer et al.): clopidogrel, ASA, and ASA + ER-DP all acceptable Class I monotherapy after non-cardioembolic ischemic stroke; PRoFESS cited as evidence of broadly similar efficacy with differing side-effect profile.
- Funder: Boehringer Ingelheim (manufacturer of telmisartan; Aggrenox licensor) sponsored and managed data. Disclose sponsor role on any NeuroWiki surface.
- Stopped early? No — ran to pre-specified 1,715 events.

## 9. Lineage placement

CAPRIE (1996) → ESPS-2 (1996) → **PRoFESS (2008)** — head-to-head of two leading monotherapies, NI failed but curves overlap, ASA–ERDP has more ICH/headache discontinuation → CHANCE (2013) / POINT (2018) pivot to short-course DAPT for minor stroke/TIA → THALES (2020) ticagrelor+ASA in same niche.

PRoFESS = the trial that operationally establishes clopidogrel and ASA–ERDP as long-term monotherapy alternatives with distinct safety profiles. NOT a trial about DAPT or the acute window.
