## 2. Population

ECASS III enrolled **821 patients** from **130 sites across 19 European countries** between July 29, 2003 and November 13, 2007.

**Key inclusion criteria (Table 1, p. 1320):**
- Acute ischemic stroke
- Age **18 to 80 years**
- Symptom onset **3 to 4.5 hours** prior to study-drug administration (window extended from 3–4h to 3–4.5h after first 228 patients via May 2005 protocol amendment)
- Symptoms ≥30 minutes without significant improvement before treatment

**Key exclusion criteria (Table 1, p. 1320):**
- Intracranial hemorrhage on imaging
- Time of symptom onset unknown
- Rapidly improving / minor symptoms
- **Severe stroke: clinical NIHSS > 25** or imaging-defined severe stroke (>1/3 MCA territory)
- Seizure at stroke onset
- Stroke or serious head trauma within previous 3 months
- **Combination of previous stroke AND diabetes mellitus**
- Heparin within 48h with elevated aPTT
- Platelets <100,000/mm³
- SBP > 185 mmHg or DBP > 110 mmHg (or aggressive IV antihypertensive needed to maintain limits)
- Glucose <50 or >400 mg/dL
- **Oral anticoagulant treatment** (regardless of INR)
- Major surgery / severe trauma within previous 3 months
- Other major bleeding-risk disorders

**Baseline characteristics (Table 2, p. 1322):**
- Mean age ~65 years (alteplase 64.9 ± 12.2; placebo 65.6 ± 11.0)
- Median NIHSS = 9 (mean ~10.7–11.6); skews toward **milder strokes** than NINDS
- Median time to treatment: 3h 59 min (alteplase), 3h 58 min (placebo)
- Treatment timing distribution: 9.6% at 3.0–3.5h, 46.8% at 3.5–4h, 39.2% at 4.0–4.5h (alteplase arm)
- Imbalance: alteplase arm had fewer patients with prior stroke (7.7% vs 14.1%, P=0.003) — post hoc adjusted analysis performed

**Generalizability to NeuroWiki population (neurology residents at the bedside):**
- Eligible: AIS patients 18–80, 3–4.5h from onset, mild-to-moderate NIHSS, no anticoagulation, no prior-stroke-plus-diabetes
- **Does NOT establish efficacy/safety for:** age >80, NIHSS >25, oral anticoagulant users, patients with both prior stroke and diabetes. AHA/ASA 2026 guidance addresses these excluded subgroups separately.

## 3. Intervention and comparator

Verbatim from Methods, "Randomization and Treatment" (p. 1319):

- **Intervention:** "0.9 mg of alteplase (Actilyse, Boehringer Ingelheim) per kilogram, administered intravenously (with an upper limit of 90 mg) … Of the total dose, 10% was administered as a bolus, and the remainder was given by continuous intravenous infusion over a period of 60 minutes."
- **Comparator:** Matched **placebo** "reconstituted from a lyophilized powder in sterile water for injection."
- **Allocation:** 1:1 via interactive voice-randomization system, randomization blocked within centers in blocks of four; block size withheld from investigators.
- **Concomitant therapy restrictions:** IV heparin, oral anticoagulants, aspirin, or volume expanders (hetastarch/dextrans) prohibited during the first 24 hours after study drug. Prophylactic subcutaneous heparin ≤10,000 IU or equivalent LMWH permitted for DVT prophylaxis.

## 4. Primary endpoint

Verbatim from Methods, "Outcome Measures" (p. 1321):

> "The primary efficacy end point was disability at day 90 (3-month visit), as assessed by means of the modified Rankin scale, dichotomized as a favorable outcome (a score of 0 or 1) or an unfavorable outcome (a score of 2 to 6)."

- **Time horizon:** 90 days
- **Dichotomization:** mRS 0–1 (favorable) vs mRS 2–6 (unfavorable)
- **Analysis population:** intention-to-treat (with worst-possible-outcome imputation for patients known to be alive but with missing day-90 data)

---

## 5. Statistical framework

**Primary design type: `superiority`**

- Binary primary endpoint, two-sided alpha of 0.05, chi-square test of proportions
- Sample size: 400/group powered at 90% to detect OR 1.4 for favorable outcome, based on pooled-analysis (Hacke 2004) data
- Pre-specified primary analysis: unadjusted chi-square
- Pre-specified secondary analysis: global outcome (Wald-type test via GEE-based linear logistic regression combining mRS 0–1, Barthel ≥95, NIHSS 0–1, GOS 1)
- Post hoc adjusted logistic regression and post hoc Cochran–Mantel–Haenszel stratified mRS shift analysis also performed (not the primary framework)

This is a **frequentist superiority RCT with a binary primary outcome**, which is the design supporting NNT calculation (see §6 below).

## 6. Primary result

**Primary endpoint (mRS 0–1 at 90 days, ITT, unadjusted; Table 3, p. 1323 and Results p. 1324):**

| Arm | Favorable mRS 0–1 / N | % |
|---|---|---|
| Alteplase | 219 / 418 | **52.4%** |
| Placebo | 182 / 403 | **45.2%** |

- **Absolute risk difference:** +7.2 percentage points (alteplase – placebo)
- **Odds ratio:** **1.34 (95% CI 1.02–1.76)**
- **Relative risk:** 1.16 (95% CI 1.01–1.34)
- **p-value:** **P = 0.04** (chi-square)
- **NNT (from pre-specified primary binary outcome):** 1 / 0.072 ≈ **14** (paper text, p. 1327: "the number needed to treat is 14 with the extended time window")

**Pre-specified secondary global outcome (ITT):** OR 1.28 (95% CI 1.00–1.65), P < 0.05.

**Post hoc CMH-adjusted mRS shift analysis (ITT, adjusted for baseline NIHSS and OTT):** P = 0.02 (paper Results, p. 1324).

**Post hoc multivariate logistic regression for mRS 0–1, ITT, adjusted for baseline imbalances:** OR 1.42 (95% CI 1.02–1.98), P = 0.04.

**Per-protocol primary endpoint (N = 730):** alteplase 54.9% vs placebo 45.4%, OR 1.47 (95% CI 1.10–1.97), P = 0.01.

## 7. Key safety results

**Symptomatic ICH (Table 5, p. 1326):**

| sICH definition | Alteplase | Placebo | OR (95% CI) | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ECASS III (primary)** | 10/418 (**2.4%**) | 1/403 (**0.2%**) | **9.85 (1.26–77.32)** | **0.008** |
| ECASS II | 22 (5.3%) | 9 (2.2%) | 2.43 (1.11–5.35) | 0.02 |
| SITS-MOST | 8 (1.9%) | 1 (0.2%) | 7.84 (0.98–63.00) | 0.02 |
| NINDS | 33 (7.9%) | 14 (3.5%) | 2.38 (1.25–4.52) | 0.006 |

- **Any ICH:** 27.0% alteplase vs 17.6% placebo, OR 1.73 (1.24–2.42), P = 0.001
- **Fatal ICH:** 3 (0.7%) alteplase vs 0 placebo
- **Symptomatic edema:** 6.9% alteplase vs 7.2% placebo, P = 0.89 (no difference)
- **Mortality at 90 days:** **32/418 (7.7%) alteplase vs 34/403 (8.4%) placebo**, OR 0.90 (0.54–1.49), **P = 0.68** — no significant difference
- Other serious adverse events: no significant differences by organ system

**Clinical takeaway:** sICH risk increased ~10-fold by ECASS III definition (absolute increase ~2.2 percentage points), without a corresponding mortality penalty — consistent with prior IVT trials.

## 8. Expert and editorial caveats

- **Trial milder than NINDS:** Median NIHSS 9 in ECASS III vs ~14 in NINDS. The paper itself (Discussion p. 1327) acknowledges this likely contributed to the higher placebo-arm favorable-outcome rate and the smaller absolute effect size relative to NINDS.
- **Effect size decay with time:** Pooled-analysis OR for 0–90 min was 2.81, 91–180 min was 1.55, vs 1.34 in ECASS III for 181–270 min — "early treatment remains essential" (Discussion p. 1327). The authors explicitly warn: "Having more time does not mean we should be allowed to take more time."
- **Excluded populations cannot be extrapolated:** age >80, NIHSS >25, OAC users, prior-stroke-plus-diabetes — efficacy/safety not established by this trial in these groups.
- **Industry sponsorship:** Funded by **Boehringer Ingelheim** (alteplase manufacturer). Multiple authors disclose consulting/lecture fees from BI; two statisticians (Bluhmki, Medeghri) and one author (Machnig) are BI employees. Independent external statistician (STATMED, Lyon) performed parallel analyses. Disclosed transparently.
- **Protocol amendment:** Window extended from 3–4h to 3–4.5h after 228 patients enrolled (May 2005), motivated by Hacke 2004 pooled analysis and slow recruitment. Not concealed; described in Methods.
- **AHA/ASA guideline incorporation:** Trial supports the 3–4.5h IV alteplase window. Per AHA/ASA 2019 update (Powers et al.) and continued through 2026 guideline: **COR 1 (Class I), LOE B-R** for IV alteplase 3–4.5h in eligible patients meeting ECASS III criteria; **COR 2b** for patients excluded by ECASS III criteria (age >80, OAC with INR ≤1.7, NIHSS >25, prior stroke + diabetes) on individualized basis. **Confirm exact 2026 wording with `stroke-guidelines` skill before authoring product copy.**
- **Subsequent reanalysis:** A 2014 IST-3-era pooled IPD meta-analysis (Emberson et al., Lancet 2014) reinforced ECASS III's time-window finding and supported benefit through 4.5h.
- **No subsequent erratum** materially altering primary results.
