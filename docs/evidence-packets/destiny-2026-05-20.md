# Evidence Packet — DESTINY Trial

**Date:** 2026-05-20
**Verifier:** evidence-verifier (clinical-trial-audit skill)
**Verification confidence:** High — full PDF of primary publication read (pp.2518–2525); DOI resolves; primary endpoint, sequential-design structure, and statistics extracted verbatim from Methods (pp.2519–2520) and Results (pp.2520–2521, Table 4).

## 1. Canonical citation
- Title: "Decompressive Surgery for the Treatment of Malignant Infarction of the Middle Cerebral Artery (DESTINY): A Randomized, Controlled Trial"
- First author: Jüttler E, for the DESTINY Study Group
- Journal: Stroke
- Vol/Issue/Pages: 38(9):2518–2525
- Year: 2007 (Received Feb 22, 2007; final revision Apr 27, 2007; accepted May 3, 2007)
- DOI: 10.1161/STROKEAHA.107.485649 (resolves)
- PMID: 17690310
- ISRCTN: ISRCTN01258591
- Sites: 6 German university hospitals (Heidelberg, Mannheim, Leipzig, Greifswald, Würzburg, Cologne)

## 2. Population
- n: 32 randomized (17 surgery, 15 conservative); 126 screened, 48 eligible, 32 consented. Sample-size projection for primary functional endpoint = 188; trial stopped at 32 after mortality boundary reached and pooled-analysis data emerged (April 2006).
- Country: Germany only
- Enrollment: Feb 2004 – Oct 2005
- Inclusion (verbatim, Table 1, p.2519):
  - Age 18–60 years
  - Clinical signs of MCA infarction with NIHSS >18 (nondominant) or >20 (dominant)
  - Decrease in level of consciousness to score ≥1 on NIHSS item 1a
  - CT-documented unilateral MCA infarction ≥⅔ territory including basal ganglia, ± anterior or posterior cerebral artery involvement
  - Symptom onset >12 and <36 hours before possible surgery
  - Treatment/surgery within 6 hours of randomization
- Key exclusions: pre-stroke mRS ≥2; pre-stroke Barthel <95; GCS <6; bilateral fixed dilated pupils; space-occupying hemorrhagic transformation; life expectancy <3 years; coagulopathy.
- Baseline (Table 3, p.2521): mean age 43.2 (surgery) vs 46.1 (conservative); P=0.44. **Median NIHSS 21 vs 24, P<0.01 (imbalance — conservative arm sicker).** Dominant hemisphere infarct: 53% vs 73% (P=0.23). Time to treatment: ~24h both arms.

**Generalizability flags:**
- Age ceiling 60y — does NOT apply to >60y (DESTINY II handles 61–82y).
- Heidelberg + Mannheim contributed 81% of patients → **"oligocenter" trial** (authors' own term, p.2524).
- Statistically significant baseline NIHSS imbalance favoring surgery (lower median in surgery arm) — confounds outcome interpretation in this small sample.
- 2 protocol violations: 1 patient randomized to medical underwent surgery beyond protocol window; 1 surgical patient never received hemicraniectomy. Both included in ITT.

## 3. Intervention and comparator
**Treatment arm** (Table 2, p.2520): standardized hemicraniectomy:
- Large reversed question-mark skin incision
- Bone-flap diameter ≥12 cm including frontal, parietal, temporal, parts of occipital squama
- Additional temporal bone removed to expose floor of middle cerebral fossa
- Dural opening + dural patch (homologous periost or temporal fascia)
- **No resection of infarcted brain tissue**
- ICP sensor inserted
- Cranioplasty 6–8 weeks post-op

**Control arm**: standardized intensive medical management — osmotherapy (mannitol/glycerol/hydroxyethyl starch), intubation criteria (GCS <8, hypoxia), hyperventilation as last resort (PaCO₂ target 28–32 mmHg), invasive ICP monitoring, propofol sedation, normothermia target, glucose 80–110 mg/dL, LMWH VTE prophylaxis. **Both arms ICU-managed, mechanically ventilated.**

## 4. Primary endpoint (VERBATIM, pp.2519–2520)

**This is the critical correction this packet documents.**

Direct quote (p.2519, second column): "DESTINY was based on a sequential design, taking mortality after 30 days as the first, **but not the primary**, efficacy end point."

Direct quote (p.2519): "patient enrollment would be interrupted until the 6-month functional outcome (**primary end point, dichotomized between mRS score 0 to 3 versus 4 to 6**) had been assessed."

Direct quote, Discussion (p.2523): "the primary end point in DESTINY was not mortality but functional outcome according to the mRS after 6 months."

**Primary endpoint:** mRS 0–3 vs mRS 4–6 at 6 months (binary functional outcome).
**First sequential endpoint (gatekeeper, NOT primary):** 30-day mortality.

**Time horizon:** 6 months for primary; 30 days for first sequential checkpoint.

## 5. Statistical framework
**Primary design type:** `binary-superiority` (with sequential gatekeeper)

- Two-stage sequential design (PEST 2.2 software):
  1. First stage powered for 30-day mortality (assumed 85% control vs 40% surgical, α=0.05, power 90%) → recalculate sample size based on observed mortality.
  2. Continue to functional primary on mRS 0–3 vs 4–6 at 6 months. Recalculated target = 188 patients (94/arm) based on assumed 20% difference.
- Two-sided χ² test, α=0.05 for primary analysis.
- ITT + per-protocol analyses (case adjudication committee).
- Single-investigator, blinded outcome assessment at 6 and 12 months.

## 6. Primary result (verbatim, Table 4 p.2522, Figure 1 p.2523)

**Primary functional endpoint (mRS 0–3 at 6 months): NOT statistically significant — underpowered.**
- Surgery: 47% (8/17) vs Conservative: 27% (4/15); **P=0.23**; difference 20%; OR 2.44 (95% CI 0.55–10.83).
- At 12 months: identical — 47% vs 27%, P=0.23.

**First sequential endpoint (30-day mortality): boundary reached after 32 patients.**
- Surgery survival 88% (15/17; 95% CI 64–99) vs Conservative survival 47% (7/15; 95% CI 21–73). Mortality 12% vs 53%. **P=0.02.** Median unbiased OR 6.37 (95% CI 1.35–29.17).
- 82% (surgery) vs 47% (conservative) alive at 6 and 12 months (P=0.03; OR 5.33, 95% CI 1.07–26.61).

**Secondary mRS 0–4 (less restrictive functional cutoff): P=0.01, OR 6.50 (95% CI 1.38–30.68)** — favors surgery.

**Why trial stopped:** Steering committee terminated April 2006 after (a) 30-day mortality boundary reached, (b) baseline NIHSS imbalance unlikely to balance further, (c) pooled-analysis data from DECIMAL + DESTINY + HAMLET emerging, (d) sample-size projection for functional primary = 188 patients deemed "no longer justifiable" given pooled evidence.

## 7. Key safety results
- Of 14 surgical survivors: 1 (7%) had mRS 5 at 6 months; 2 of 7 conservative survivors (28%) had mRS 5.
- Surgical-arm late death: 1 patient died day 157 from fatal post-op pulmonary embolism (after craniectomy + cranioplasty).
- All other surgical deaths within 8 days post-op.
- Conservative-arm deaths: all within first 30 days from herniation/brain edema.
- No "vegetative state" (mRS 5) increase in surgery arm — directly addresses pre-trial concern that surgery would create more severely disabled survivors.
- Per p.2523: "of the 14 survivors in the surgical arm, only 1 showed an mRS of 5 (7%) compared with 2 of 7 survivors in the conservative treatment arm (28%)" — surgery did NOT increase the "fate worse than death" outcome.

## 8. Expert and editorial caveats

### 8a. Accompanying editorial in Stroke Sept 2007
**No dedicated editorial accompanying DESTINY was identified** in Stroke Vol 38 Issue 9 via PubMed search. DESTINY and DECIMAL were paired publications (pp.2506–2525 in the same issue) but no editorial was published with them. The de facto editorial-equivalent is Vahedi et al.'s pooled analysis in Lancet Neurology one month earlier (§8d). State explicitly: no Stroke-issue editorial.

### 8b. Letters / correspondence within 12 months
PubMed search for Stroke 2007–2008 letters citing DESTINY: no qualifying correspondence. The DESTINY group itself published DESTINY II (2014) addressing the principal critique (age ≤60 ceiling).

### 8c. Guideline incorporation (decisive)
**AHA/ASA 2018 Guidelines for the Early Management of Acute Ischemic Stroke (Powers et al., Stroke 2018) → Class IIa, Level of Evidence A** for decompressive craniectomy in patients ≤60y with malignant MCA infarction deteriorating despite medical therapy. DESTINY is one of the three foundational RCTs cited, primarily through its inclusion in the Vahedi 2007 pooled analysis. Recommendation maintained in the 2019 update.

Notably, DESTINY's pre-specified intent was to demonstrate mortality reduction "without an unacceptable increase in severely disabled survivors" — and on that question, it succeeded (no increase in mRS 5; numerically more mRS 2–4 survivors). This is the bedrock argument for the Class IIa rating.

### 8d. Most important follow-up
**Vahedi K, Hofmeijer J, Juettler E, et al. "Early decompressive surgery in malignant infarction of the middle cerebral artery: a pooled analysis of three randomised controlled trials." Lancet Neurol. 2007;6(3):215–222. DOI: 10.1016/S1474-4422(07)70036-4. PMID: 17303527.**

- Pre-specified pooled IPD analysis of DECIMAL + DESTINY + HAMLET (≤48h subset), N=93.
- Probability of survival rose from 22% to 71%; survival with mRS ≤4 rose from 24% to 75%; survival with mRS ≤3 nearly doubled; mRS 5 not increased.
- Provides the precision DESTINY alone (N=32) could not.

**DESTINY II (Jüttler E et al., NEJM 2014;370:1091–1100; DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa1311367; PMID 24645942)** — same group, extended to 61–82y. Already cataloged separately in NeuroWiki.
