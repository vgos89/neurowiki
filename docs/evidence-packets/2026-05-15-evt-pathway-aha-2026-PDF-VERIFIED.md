## Section 4.7.3 — Posterior Circulation Stroke

| # | COR | LOE | Recommendation (verbatim) |
|---|-----|-----|---------------------------|
| 1 | **1** | **A** | "In patients with AIS, with basilar artery occlusion, a baseline mRS score of 0 to 1, NIHSS score ≥10 at presentation, and PC-ASPECTS ≥6 (mild ischemic damage), EVT within 24 hours from onset of symptoms is recommended to achieve better functional outcome and reduce mortality." |
| 2 | **2b** | **B-R** | "In patients with AIS, with basilar artery occlusion, a baseline mRS score of 0 to 1, NIHSS score 6 to 9 at presentation, and PC-ASPECTS ≥6 (mild ischemic damage), the effectiveness of EVT within 24 hours to improve functional outcomes and reduce mortality is not well established." |

**Supporting text on NIHSS 6–9 stratum (page e57, Rec-Specific text item 2, verbatim):** "Due to low enrollment in the BAOCHE RCT, following the 61st patient, the investigators expanded the inclusion criteria to include patients with NIHSS scores ≥6. Data are limited for patients with stroke severity NIHSS scores of 6 to 9, as only 17 patients were randomized, of whom 6 received EVT. All patients treated with EVT reached the trial defined good outcome (mRS score, 0–3), while only 6 of the 11 managed medically got these results. Globally, the study showed that in the enrolled patients who met radiographic criteria of a PC-ASPECTS score ≥6 without a pons-midbrain index ≥3 (indicating extensive infarction in the brainstem region), EVT showed favorable outcomes that reached statistical significance, over the medical management group (mRS of 0–3 was achieved in 46% of the EVT group, as opposed to 24% in the medical management group). A meta-analysis of the 4 RCTs (BEST, BASICS, ATTENTION, and BAOCHE) showed the median NIHSS of ~20; however, functional outcome differences may exist among patients with basilar occlusions presenting with a low NIHSS score followed by neurological decline, as opposed to high NIHSS scores at the outset. Therefore, future investigation of EVT in patients with an NIHSS stroke severity score of 6 to 9 at presentation is warranted."

## Resolution of the 7 PDF-gated audit findings

### Finding 1 — Basilar NIHSS 6–9 stratum

- **Verbatim 2026 text (Section 4.7.3 Rec 2):** *"In patients with AIS, with basilar artery occlusion, a baseline mRS score of 0 to 1, NIHSS score 6 to 9 at presentation, and PC-ASPECTS ≥6 (mild ischemic damage), the effectiveness of EVT within 24 hours to improve functional outcomes and reduce mortality is not well established."*
- **Class / LOE:** **COR 2b / LOE B-R**
- **Figure 3 cross-check:** Basilar / 0–24 h / PC-ASPECTS ≥6 / NIHSS 6–9 → orange **2b**. Confirmed.
- **Resolution:** Codebase's "Class IIb / NIHSS 6–9 / pc-ASPECTS ≥6" branch **stands**. NIHSS ≥10 is Class I A; NIHSS 6–9 is a separate Class IIb B-R branch. These are NOT under the same umbrella — they are two separately numbered recommendations.
- **Recommended action for codebase:** **Keep** the existing Class IIb branch. Confirm the LOE is rendered as "B-R" (not "B-NR" or "C"). Confirm the time window says "within 24 hours" (not 12 h) — BAOCHE was 24 h, ATTENTION was 12 h, but the recommendation language is "within 24 hours."

---

### Finding 3 — Basilar prestroke mRS 2

- **Verbatim 2026 text:** No Section 4.7.3 recommendation addresses basilar EVT for prestroke mRS ≥2. Both numbered recommendations (Rec 1 NIHSS ≥10 and Rec 2 NIHSS 6–9) specify "baseline mRS score of 0 to 1."
- **Figure 3 cross-check (page e61):** Under basilar artery → PC-ASPECTS ≥6 → the algorithm shows two stroke severity branches (NIHSS ≥10 → EVT 1; NIHSS 6–9 → EVT 2b) and a separate "mRS ≥2" branch → **IDD (insufficient data to determine).**
- **Class / LOE:** No class assigned. Maps to "Insufficient Data to Determine" — i.e., no recommendation either for or against.
- **Resolution:** The packet's secondary source previously suggesting basilar mRS 2 might be Class IIa **was wrong**. 2026 does NOT extend any positive class to basilar EVT for prestroke mRS ≥2. ATTENTION enrolled a small number of mRS 2 patients, but the guideline's explicit pre-stroke mRS specification is "0 to 1" in both Rec 1 and Rec 2.
- **Recommended action for codebase:** If trialData.ts/EVT pathway currently shows basilar prestroke mRS 2 as Class IIa or any positive class, **soften to "insufficient data — individualized decision"** (matches the IDD designation in Figure 3). Do NOT label it Class IIa.

### Finding 8 — Anterior 6–24 h "Class IIb large core 50–100 mL" branch

- **Verbatim 2026 text:** No separate "50–100 mL" or "moderate-large core" stratum exists in Section 4.7.2 for the 6–24 h window. The 6–24 h window is partitioned by ASPECTS only: ASPECTS 6–10 (Rec 2, Class I A) and ASPECTS 3–5 (Rec 3, Class I A with age <80 + no significant mass effect).
- **Figure 3 cross-check:** 6–24 h branch has only two ASPECTS strata: 6–10 and 3–5. There is no ASPECTS 0–2 branch in the 6–24 h pathway. Both 6–10 and 3–5 are Class **1** for mRS 0–1.
- **Class / LOE for ASPECTS 3–5 at 6–24 h:** **COR 1 / LOE A** (with age <80, NIHSS ≥6, mRS 0–1, no mass effect).
- **Resolution:** The codebase's "Class IIb large core 50–100 mL" stratum in the 6–24 h pathway **does not match the 2026 guideline**. 2026 collapses large-core selection at 6–24 h into a Class I A recommendation for ASPECTS 3–5 (no separate volumetric branch). There is no Class IIb tier at 6–24 h based on core volume.
- **Recommended action for codebase:** **Rewrite.** Either (a) replace the "50–100 mL Class IIb" stratum with the 2026 ASPECTS 3–5 Class I A stratum, OR (b) merge any volumetric language into the imaging caveats and remove the standalone IIb branch. Either way, the IIb label at 6–24 h based on core volume is wrong and should not stand.

### Finding 9 — Anterior 6–24 h core >100 mL ("Avoid EVT" in codebase)

- **Verbatim 2026 text:** No explicit upper core volume cap (e.g., ">100 mL → do not treat") appears in Section 4.7.2 recommendations. The 6–24 h window terminates at ASPECTS 3 (no ASPECTS 0–2 branch in 6–24 h per Figure 3).
- **Caveat verbatim (page e54 footnote and page e55 supporting text):** SELECT2 exploratory analysis identified "a threshold of ≥26 mL of severe CT hypodensity, defined as the lower 99% CI of the contralateral thalamic gray matter (≤26 Hounsfield units), was associated with diminished treatment benefit from EVT. Patients with CT hypodensity above this threshold derived no functional benefit and instead experienced increased risks, including cerebral edema and the need for hemicraniectomy." This is a *hypodensity-volume* threshold, not a total-core threshold, and it is presented as caveat language, not a numbered Class III "Harm" recommendation.
- **Figure 3 cross-check:** 6–24 h pathway has no ASPECTS 0–2 branch at all → IDD by absence (the algorithm does not extend EVT recommendations to ASPECTS 0–2 in the late window).
- **Class / LOE:** No COR 3:Harm exists for "core >100 mL" in any window. The 0–6 h window does have a Class IIa B-R recommendation for ASPECTS 0–2 (Rec 4). For 6–24 h with ASPECTS 0–2, the guideline is silent (IDD) — neither recommend nor harm.
- **Resolution:** The codebase's "Avoid EVT if core >100 mL at 6–24 h" framing **overstates** the 2026 position. 2026 does not contain an explicit anti-recommendation; it simply does not extend the late-window evidence to ASPECTS 0–2. The SELECT2 ≥26 mL severe-hypodensity caveat is **the only volume-based caveat** and applies as a clinical risk-stratification tool, not as a Class III rule.
- **Recommended action for codebase:** **Soften.** Replace "Avoid EVT" with "Insufficient evidence; consider individualized risk discussion, particularly noting SELECT2 finding of diminished benefit and increased complications when severe CT hypodensity volume is ≥26 mL." Do NOT label this Class III "Harm." **This is the patient-safety-significant correction.**

## Newly discovered nuances not in the secondary-source packet

1. **Two-tier prestroke mRS structure at 0–6 h.** 2026 has separate recommendations for prestroke mRS 2 (Rec 5, Class IIa B-NR) and prestroke mRS 3–4 (Rec 6, Class IIb B-NR). Both require ASPECTS ≥6. The packet's earlier secondary source conflated these. Codebase should have two distinct branches, not one.

2. **The SELECT2 ≥26 mL severe-hypodensity caveat is volumetric on a specific HU threshold**, not on total core volume. It is presented as a footnote, not a numbered Class III recommendation. The codebase should not treat it as a hard contraindication, only as a counseling point during shared decision-making.

3. **ASPECTS 3–5 at 6–24 h carries an age <80 stipulation that ASPECTS 6–10 does not.** Rec 3 (6–24 h, ASPECTS 3–5) explicitly states "age <80 years." Rec 2 (6–24 h, ASPECTS 6–10) does not impose an age cap. The codebase should not apply the <80 cap broadly to all late-window patients.

4. **Mass-effect exclusion.** Both Rec 3 (6–24 h ASPECTS 3–5) and Rec 4 (0–6 h ASPECTS 0–2) require "without significant mass effect on imaging." This is a non-quantitative imaging criterion that should be surfaced in the pathway.

5. **General-anesthesia vs procedural-sedation equivalence (4.7.4 Rec 3, COR 1 B-R).** Either is recommended. The codebase should not signal a preference for one over the other based on older literature.

6. **Adjunctive intra-arterial thrombolytic after successful EVT (4.7.4 Rec 8, COR 2b B-R).** New in 2026: post-mTICI 2b/3 IA urokinase/alteplase/tenecteplase may be reasonable.
