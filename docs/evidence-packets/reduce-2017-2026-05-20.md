## Citation

- **Author:** Søndergaard L, Kasner SE, Rhodes JF, et al. for the Gore REDUCE Clinical Study Investigators
- **Title:** Patent Foramen Ovale Closure or Antiplatelet Therapy for Cryptogenic Stroke
- **Journal:** N Engl J Med 2017;377(11):1033–1042
- **DOI:** 10.1056/NEJMoa1707404
- **PMID:** 28902580
- **ClinicalTrials.gov:** NCT00738894
- **Published:** September 14, 2017

## Trial identity

- Multinational, prospective, randomized, controlled, open-label superiority trial with blinded outcome adjudication.
- 63 sites: Canada, Denmark, Finland, Norway, Sweden, UK, US.
- Enrollment December 2008 to February 2015.
- Funded by W.L. Gore and Associates (the device sponsor). Author independence statement: sponsor had no role in writing or submission.

## Population

- 664 patients, ages **18–59**, mean age 45.2 years.
- Cryptogenic ischemic stroke within **180 days**, confirmed by clinical syndrome ≥24h OR imaging-confirmed infarct.
- TEE-confirmed PFO with right-to-left shunt.
- Excluded: large-artery atherosclerosis (≥50% stenosis), established cardioembolic source, small-vessel/lacunar (<1.5 cm deep infarct), hypercoagulable disorder, dissection, uncontrolled diabetes/hypertension, autoimmune disease, indication for anticoagulation.
- Prolonged ECG monitoring **not required**.
- **81% had moderate or large interatrial shunts** (shunt size 6–25 or >25 microbubbles); 20.4% of the closure arm had atrial septal aneurysm (not assessed in the antiplatelet-only arm at baseline).
- Inclusion broader than CLOSE (no ASA requirement) but narrower than RESPECT (capped at age 59).

## Randomization and intervention

- **2:1** PFO closure + antiplatelet vs antiplatelet alone (441 vs 223).
- Devices: HELEX Septal Occluder (through late 2012) or Cardioform Septal Occluder (from late 2012). Both Gore devices.
- Antiplatelet (both arms, site-mandated, same in both): aspirin 75–325 mg/day, or aspirin 50–100 mg + dipyridamole 225–400 mg/day, or clopidogrel 75 mg/day. **Anticoagulation not permitted** — clean antiplatelet comparator.
- PFO closure arm: clopidogrel 300 mg loading + 75 mg/day for 3 days post-procedure, then chosen antiplatelet for trial duration.
- Median follow-up: 3.2 years (IQR 2.2–4.8).

## Primary outcome and result

- **Two coprimary endpoints:**
  - (1) Freedom from clinical ischemic stroke through ≥24 months
  - (2) 24-month incidence of new brain infarction (composite of clinical ischemic stroke OR silent brain infarction on T2 MRI)
- **Coprimary 1 — Clinical ischemic stroke (ITT):**
  - 6/441 (1.4%) PFO closure vs 12/223 (5.4%) antiplatelet-only
  - Event rates: 0.39 vs 1.71 strokes per 100 patient-years
  - **HR 0.23 (95% CI 0.09–0.62), P = 0.002 by log-rank**
  - Multiplicity-adjusted P = 0.002.
  - Authors-reported NNT to prevent 1 stroke over 24 months: **~28** (Discussion p.1040).
- **Coprimary 2 — New brain infarction at 24 months:**
  - 18/383 (4.7%) PFO closure vs 19/177 (10.7%) antiplatelet-only
  - **RR 0.44 (95% CI 0.24–0.81), P = 0.02**; multiplicity-adjusted P = 0.048.
- **Silent brain infarction alone:** no significant difference (3.4% vs 4.0%, P = 0.75). The clinical-stroke component drove the composite.

## Safety — atrial fibrillation signal (prominent)

- **Atrial fibrillation or flutter: 29/441 (6.6%) PFO closure vs 1/223 (0.4%) antiplatelet-only, P < 0.001.** Largest absolute AF signal of the three trials.
- 83% of AF cases detected within 45 days of procedure; 59% resolved within 2 weeks. Predominantly transient peri-procedural AF.
- 1/29 patients with post-closure AF had a recurrent stroke.
- Serious device-related adverse events: 6/441 (1.4%) — device dislocation (3), device-related thrombosis (2), aortic dissection (1).
- Major bleeding: 1.8% PFO closure vs 2.7% antiplatelet (P = 0.57).
- Deaths in PFO closure: 1 suicide (day 131) + 1 fatal MI (day 1045). None in antiplatelet-only group.

## Statistical design

- **primaryDesign: binary-superiority** (time-to-event clinical stroke, log-rank).
- **primaryResult: met** (HR 0.23, P = 0.002).
- NNT ~28 over 24 months is interpretable here.
- Two coprimary endpoints with Dubey–Armitage–Parmar multiplicity adjustment.
- Initially powered as one-sided with planned interim analysis; revised mid-trial (without unblinding) to two coprimary endpoints after CLOSURE-I/PC/RESPECT reported lower-than-expected event rates; interim analysis rescinded. Documented in PDF Methods and acknowledged as a limitation.

## Subgroup heterogeneity (exploratory)

- Effect homogeneous across age (18–45 vs 46–59), sex, and geography (Europe/Canada vs US).
- Strongest signal in moderate-to-large shunt subgroup (HR 0.18, P = 0.001) vs small shunt (HR 0.27, P = 0.26 — wide CI from small numbers).
- ASA presence not analyzable in the antiplatelet arm (only assessed at procedure in closure arm).

## §8 editorial context

### §8a Accompanying NEJM editorial
- Ropper AH. Tipping Point for Patent Foramen Ovale Closure. N Engl J Med 2017;377(11):1093–1095.
- For REDUCE specifically: Ropper highlighted that REDUCE's **clean antiplatelet comparator** (no anticoagulation crossover) and its strong HR 0.23 helped resolve the ambiguity of the 2012–2013 trials. He also flagged the prominent atrial fibrillation signal (6.6%) as the central safety question going forward — whether procedure-induced transient AF carries the same stroke risk as substrate-driven persistent AF remains unresolved.

### §8b Post-publication NEJM letters
- Shared methodological critiques across the cluster (see CLOSE packet §8b). For REDUCE specifically: (1) higher-than-expected event rate in antiplatelet-only arm (5.4% vs ~3% projected) may reflect enrichment for high-risk patients or earlier post-stroke enrollment; (2) silent infarction MRI methodology used T2 sequences with a 3-mm lesion floor — current practice uses higher field strength and DWI/FLAIR, possibly explaining the negative silent-infarct subcomponent; (3) 6.3% of antiplatelet-only patients crossed over to closure during the trial — selection bias toward null.

### §8c Subsequent guideline incorporation
- **AHA/ASA 2021 Secondary Prevention Guideline** (Kleindorfer DO et al., Stroke 2021;52:e364–e467): Class IIa, Level B-R for PFO closure in non-lacunar stroke <60 with PFO and no other apparent etiology. CLOSE, RESPECT, and REDUCE cited together.
- **FDA approval (March 2018):** Gore Cardioform Septal Occluder approved for PFO closure in cryptogenic stroke (this device tested in REDUCE).
- **AAN 2020 Practice Advisory** (Messé SR et al., Neurology 2020;94:876–885).
- **ESO 2019 Guideline** (Pristipino C et al., Eur Stroke J 2019).

### §8d Subsequent meta-analyses
- Mir H et al. BMJ 2018;362:k2515 — IPD meta-analysis incorporating REDUCE: pooled HR 0.36 for recurrent stroke; AF OR ~4.7.
- Kent DM et al. **PASCAL classification** (JAMA 2021;326:2277–2286): IPD pooling stratifying patients into unlikely/possible/probable PFO-attributable strokes.
- Subsequent Cochrane reviews and 2024 ESC/ESO updates have not materially changed the conclusion. The atrial-fibrillation signal seen most prominently in REDUCE prompted further interest in whether the procedure-induced AF reflects true new substrate or transient irritation; longitudinal follow-up data (including REDUCE's 4.4-year median) suggest it is largely transient.

## Verbatim quoted text (for registry)

> "Among patients with a PFO who had had a cryptogenic stroke, the risk of subsequent ischemic stroke was lower among those assigned to PFO closure combined with antiplatelet therapy than among those assigned to antiplatelet therapy alone; however, PFO closure was associated with higher rates of device complications and atrial fibrillation."

(NEJM 2017;377:1033, Conclusions, abstract p.1033)
