# Evidence packet — Sarode 2013 (4F-PCC vs FFP for VKA reversal)

**Author:** medical-scientist
**Date:** 2026-05-21
**Status:** verified against PubMed (PMID 23935011); Circulation full text via PMC

## Citation

Sarode R, Milling TJ Jr, Refaai MA, et al. Efficacy and safety of a 4-factor prothrombin complex concentrate in patients on vitamin K antagonists presenting with major bleeding: a randomized, plasma-controlled, phase IIIb study. **Circulation 2013;128(11):1234–1243.**
- PMID: 23935011
- DOI: 10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.113.002283
- URL: https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.113.002283

## Design

- Multicenter, open-label, **noninferiority** randomized trial
- 4F-PCC (Kcentra / Beriplex P/N) vs plasma (FFP)
- 36 sites in North America and Europe
- 1:1 randomization
- Phase IIIb (supported FDA approval of Kcentra in April 2013)

## Population

- Adults on VKA (warfarin or acenocoumarol)
- Acute major bleeding requiring rapid INR reversal
- Most common bleed sites: GI (~38%), intracranial (~14%), visible (~13%)
- All patients also received vitamin K 5–10 mg IV

## Coprimary endpoints (verbatim)

"24-hour hemostatic efficacy from start of infusion and international normalized ratio correction (≤1.3) at 0.5 hour after end of infusion"

- Noninferiority margin for hemostatic efficacy: **−10 percentage points** (lower bound of 95% CI for difference)
- For INR correction: superiority pre-specified after NI established

## Intervention details

- 4F-PCC dose: weight- and INR-based (25–50 IU/kg)
- FFP dose: 10–15 mL/kg
- Both arms: vitamin K 5–10 mg IV adjunct

## Primary results

- **Hemostatic efficacy** at 24 h: 4F-PCC 71/98 (72.4%) vs FFP 68/104 (65.4%)
  - Difference +7.1 pp (95% CI −5.8 to +19.9)
  - Lower bound of CI (−5.8) above NI margin (−10): **NI established**
- **INR ≤1.3 at 0.5 h post-infusion:** 4F-PCC 61/98 (62.2%) vs FFP 10/104 (9.6%)
  - Difference +52.6 pp (95% CI 39.4 to 65.9)
  - **Superiority established** for INR target time
- Trial result: POSITIVE
- primaryDesign: noninferiority; primaryResult: noninferiority-established

## Safety

- Mortality: 5/98 (5.1%) 4F-PCC vs 5/104 (4.8%) FFP — comparable
- Thromboembolic events: 7/103 (6.8%) 4F-PCC vs 7/109 (6.4%) FFP — comparable
- Fluid overload: 3/103 (2.9%) 4F-PCC vs 13/109 (11.9%) FFP — favors 4F-PCC
- Median infusion volume: 99.4 mL 4F-PCC vs 813.5 mL FFP — order-of-magnitude difference

## Statistical framework

- Noninferiority on hemostatic efficacy with margin Δ = −10 pp
- Superiority on INR target as predefined sequential hierarchy
- Appropriate display: ni-margin-chart
- NNT not appropriate (NI primary)

## §8 Editorial context

### §8a Accompanying editorials
- Levi M. *Circulation* 2013;128(11):1179–1181 ("Hemostatic agents: making rapid progress") — endorsed 4F-PCC as preferred reversal agent; flagged that the ICH-specific subset was small.

### §8b Post-publication letters
- Concerns raised in correspondence about the small ICH subgroup (n≈28) and applicability to neurologic emergencies; Sarode et al. acknowledged this in reply (Circulation 2014).

### §8c Subsequent guideline incorporation
- **AHA/ASA 2022 ICH Guideline (Greenberg SM et al., Stroke 2022;53:e282–e361):** Section 5.2.1 — for VKA-associated ICH with elevated INR, 4F-PCC preferred over FFP (**Class I, Level A**) plus vitamin K 10 mg IV. Cites Sarode 2013 and INCH 2016 as the supporting RCT evidence base.
- **ESO 2022 ICH Guidelines:** strong recommendation for 4F-PCC over FFP in VKA-associated ICH.
- **Neurocritical Care Society Reversal Guideline 2016 (Frontera et al.):** 4F-PCC strong recommendation, FFP only if PCC unavailable.

### §8d Subsequent meta-analyses / contradicting evidence
- **INCH (Steiner T et al., Lancet Neurol 2016;15:566–573):** ICH-specific RCT (4F-PCC vs FFP); halted early at interim for INR reversal benefit favoring 4F-PCC; provides ICH-specific evidence Sarode 2013 lacked. (Full PDF pending V supply.)
- **Chai-Adisaksopha et al., Thromb Haemost 2016:** meta-analysis of 17 studies confirmed 4F-PCC superior to FFP on INR reversal and mortality in VKA reversal.
- **Milling et al., Ann Emerg Med 2016 (pooled phase 3/3b):** confirmed Sarode 2013 findings in larger pooled dataset.

## Bedside framing

For VKA-associated major bleeding (including ICH), 4F-PCC 25–50 IU/kg IV + vitamin K 10 mg IV is preferred over FFP per AHA/ASA 2022 Class I, Level A. Goal: INR <1.4 within 30 min – 4 h. Sarode 2013 is the foundational trial; INCH 2016 is the ICH-specific confirmation.
