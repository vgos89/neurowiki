# Evidence packet — ANNEXA-4 (2019)

**Author:** medical-scientist
**Date:** 2026-05-21
**Status:** verified against PubMed (PMID 30730782); PMC6699827 full text available

## Citation

Connolly SJ, Crowther M, Eikelboom JW, et al. for the ANNEXA-4 Investigators. Full Study Report of Andexanet Alfa for Bleeding Associated with Factor Xa Inhibitors. **N Engl J Med 2019;380(14):1326–1335.**
- PMID: 30730782
- PMC: PMC6699827
- DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa1814051
- URL: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6699827/

## Design

- **Single-arm, open-label prospective cohort study** (no comparator arm; no randomization)
- Conducted to support FDA accelerated approval (granted May 2018)
- 63 centers across North America and Europe
- April 2015 – May 2018

## Population

- Acute major bleeding within 18 h of FXa-inhibitor dose
- Apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban, or enoxaparin
- 352 enrolled; 254 in efficacy population

## Hemorrhage distribution

- **Intracranial:** 227/352 (64%)
- Gastrointestinal: 90/352 (26%)
- Other: 35/352 (10%)

## Primary endpoints (coprimary, both descriptive)

1. Percent change in anti-FXa activity from baseline to nadir (end of bolus)
2. Rate of excellent or good hemostatic efficacy at 12 h after infusion end

## Primary results

- **Anti-FXa activity reduction:**
  - Apixaban: median 149.7 → 11.1 ng/mL (92% reduction; 95% CI 91–93)
  - Rivaroxaban: median 211.8 → 14.2 ng/mL (92% reduction; 95% CI 88–94)
- **Hemostatic efficacy:** 204/249 (82%) achieved excellent or good hemostasis at 12 h

## Safety (30-day)

- Mortality: 49/352 (14%)
- Thrombotic events: 34/352 (10%)
- Notable: thrombotic-event rate high; not directly attributable to andexanet given single-arm design

## Statistical framework

- **single-arm-registry** design — observational cohort behind FDA approval
- No comparator → no superiority, no NI, no NNT
- Display archetype: registry-safety (no efficacy bars)
- primaryDesign: single-arm-registry; primaryResult: met (descriptive thresholds achieved → safety-threshold-met)

## Relationship to ANNEXA-I

- ANNEXA-4 (2019, this study) = observational cohort; FDA accelerated approval pathway
- **ANNEXA-I (Connolly SJ et al., NEJM 2024;390:1745–1755; PMID 38804514)** = subsequent confirmatory RCT specifically in ICH; established efficacy vs usual care AND quantified the ischemic-stroke harm trade-off
- ANNEXA-4 is the foundation for FDA approval; ANNEXA-I is the post-approval RCT confirming hemostatic activity in a randomized framework

## §8 Editorial context

### §8a Accompanying editorials
- Cushman M. *NEJM* 2019;380(14):1377–1378. "Andexanet alfa — bench to bedside to … bench." — emphasized that a single-arm trial cannot establish efficacy and the 10% thrombotic-event rate raised concern.

### §8b Post-publication letters
- Multiple letters (NEJM 2019;381) discussed dosing optimization, cost (~US$23,000–$50,000 per high-dose course), and whether 4F-PCC was a reasonable alternative comparator.

### §8c Subsequent guideline incorporation
- **AHA/ASA 2022 ICH Guideline (Greenberg SM et al., Stroke 2022;53:e282–e361):** Section 5.2.2 — andexanet alfa **Class IIa, Level B-NR** for FXa-inhibitor-associated ICH "when timely available," based on ANNEXA-4 single-arm data. 4F-PCC also Class IIb, Level C-LD as alternative.
- **ESO 2022 ICH Guidelines:** weak recommendation for andexanet OR 4F-PCC in FXa-associated ICH.
- **Neurocritical Care Society 2016 Reversal Guideline (2020 update):** andexanet preferred when available; 4F-PCC if not.

### §8d Subsequent meta-analyses / contradicting evidence
- **ANNEXA-I (Connolly 2024, PMID 38804514):** RCT in ICH specifically — confirmed hemostatic efficacy vs usual care (NNT 8 for hemostatic surrogate) but quantified an ischemic-stroke excess (6.5% vs 1.5%, NNH 20) absent from the single-arm ANNEXA-4 dataset.
- **Costa et al., Crit Care 2022** systematic review: comparable hemostatic efficacy with 4F-PCC at ~10% the cost; no head-to-head RCT.
- **Smith EE, Hemphill JC. NEJM 2024;390:1815:** editorial accompanying ANNEXA-I; cost-effectiveness and head-to-head comparison still unresolved.

## Bedside framing

ANNEXA-4 established hemostatic activity and supported FDA approval but cannot establish efficacy. Use the AHA/ASA 2022 Class IIa, Level B-NR framing. For randomized confirmation in ICH specifically, cite ANNEXA-I (2024).
