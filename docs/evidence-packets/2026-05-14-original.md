## 2. Population

- **Geography:** China — 55 neurology clinics/stroke centers (Chinese cohort only)
- **Age:** Adults ≥18 years; median 66 (IQR 58–73) TNK arm, 65 (57–73) alteplase arm; ~6% >80 years
- **Sex:** 30.4% female (males predominate: 70.6% TNK / 68.5% alteplase)
- **Stroke subtype:** Acute ischemic stroke (AIS) eligible for IV thrombolysis within 4.5 hours of symptom onset
- **NIHSS range:** 1–25 at enrollment; median 6 (IQR 5.0–9.0)
  - <6: ~40.7% · 6–15: ~53.3% · >15: ~5.9%
  - For NIHSS <4: required measurable deficit in motor function (arms/legs) for inclusion
- **Imaging selection:** Noncontrast CT at screening (to exclude ICH). CTA/MRA performed in ~25.5% at investigator discretion. **Not an imaging-selected trial.**
- **Co-enrollment / EVT:** Patients with planned endovascular thrombectomy were eligible; thrombectomy performed in 8.3% TNK / 7.4% alteplase
- **Generalizability caveats for NeuroWiki audience:**
  - Chinese cohort only — lower baseline stroke severity than AcT (median NIHSS 6 vs 9-10 in AcT)
  - Mild predominance (~41% with NIHSS <6)
  - Low LVO representation
  - Open-label design

## 3. Intervention and comparator (verbatim from methods)

> "Patients were randomized (1:1) within 4.5 hours of symptom onset to receive intravenous tenecteplase (0.25 mg/kg) or intravenous alteplase (0.9 mg/kg)."

- **Tenecteplase:** 0.25 mg/kg IV, maximum 25 mg, single bolus over 5–10 seconds (Boehringer Ingelheim)
- **Alteplase:** 0.9 mg/kg IV, maximum 90 mg; 10% as initial bolus, remainder as 1-hour infusion (Boehringer Ingelheim)
- Treatment administered within 4.5 hours of stroke symptom onset

---

## 4. Primary endpoint (verbatim from methods)

> "The primary outcome was the proportion of patients who achieved an mRS score of 0 (no symptoms at all) or 1 (no significant disability despite symptoms; able to carry out all usual duties and activities) on day 90."

- **Time horizon:** 90 days
- **Dichotomization:** mRS 0–1 (excellent functional outcome)
- **Note:** NeuroWiki must NOT display this as mRS 0–2. ORIGINAL's primary is mRS 0–1, distinct from many other thrombolysis trials.

---

## 5. Statistical framework

**Primary design:** `noninferiority`

- **NI margin (verbatim):** "a noninferiority risk ratio (RR) margin of 0.937"
- **Margin derivation:** Based on preservation of ≥50% of alteplase's effect (f=0.5; M2=1.0677) from meta-analysis of alteplase vs placebo (unadjusted RR 1.24, 95% CI 1.14–1.36); margin (1/M2) = 0.937. Corresponds to ~−3.97% absolute risk difference assuming 63% expected response rate.
- **Hierarchical testing:** Superiority tested only after NI established
- **Analysis model:** Log-binomial regression adjusted for baseline NIHSS, age, and time to drug administration; full analysis set; multiple imputation for missing primary outcome
- **Type I error:** 0.025 (1-sided); 80% power; sample size 1478 (~739/arm)
- **Margin history:** Initially −9.5% ARD (PROST-based); revised to −5% after AcT (not implemented); finalized as RR margin 0.937 per China CDE / preserved-effect framework

## 6. Primary result

| Statistic | Value |
|---|---|
| TNK mRS 0–1 at 90d | 532/732 (72.7%) |
| Alteplase mRS 0–1 at 90d | 515/733 (70.3%) |
| Adjusted RR (95% CI) | **1.03 (0.97 to 1.09)** |
| Risk difference (95% CI) | 2.12% (−2.17 to 6.40) |
| P value for noninferiority | **0.003** |
| P value for superiority | 0.37 |
| Noninferiority margin (RR) | 0.937 |
| **NI established?** | **YES** — lower bound of 95% CI (0.97) is above predefined NI margin of 0.937 |
| **Superiority established?** | **NO** — lower bound of CI for adjusted RR is not above 1 (P=0.37); the null hypothesis for superiority was not rejected |

**Trial's actual conclusion (verbatim):** "Tenecteplase was noninferior to alteplase with respect to excellent functional outcomes (mRS score of 0 or 1) at 90 days in patients with AIS within 4.5 hours of symptom onset."

**doesNotProve flag:** NI established ≠ superiority. Per trial-statistician rules, this is "NI met" — not "TNK superior." Any NeuroWiki copy that says "tenecteplase was better" is incorrect; it is "non-inferior to" or "a suitable alternative to" alteplase.

## 8. Expert and editorial caveats

- **Comparator trial context (per Discussion):**
  - **AcT (Menon et al., Lancet 2022):** Canadian pragmatic RCT, NI established, mRS 0–1 ARD 2.1% (95% CI −2.6 to 6.9). Higher baseline NIHSS (9–10) than ORIGINAL (6). Older population.
  - **TRACE-2 (Wang et al., Lancet 2023):** Chinese RCT using locally manufactured TNK (CSPC); excluded EVT-eligible patients and NIHSS ≤4. Mortality 7% TNK in TRACE-2 vs 4.6% TNK in ORIGINAL.
  - **ATTEST-2 (Muir et al., 2023):** UK RCT, RD 1.99% (95% CI −2.77 to 6.75) — consistent with ORIGINAL.
  - **TASTE (Australia, NCT-based):** ongoing/recently completed at time of publication; pending data on TNK 0.25 mg/kg in selected populations.
- **Funding/COI:** Funded by Boehringer Ingelheim (China); sponsor designed the trial, managed data, performed analyses, and had opportunity to review manuscript. Authors had final decision on submission. Two authors are BI employees. This is a notable disclosure to surface in `legend` or `howToInterpret`.
- **Open-label limitation:** Acknowledged; blinded end-point adjudication mitigates but does not eliminate post-procedural care bias.
- **Subgroup signal:** In patients >80 years, RR 2.44 (1.42–4.17) favoring TNK (treatment × age interaction P<0.05). The authors note "the sample size was too small to draw meaningful conclusions" — must NOT be displayed as a primary claim.
- **AHA/ASA guideline status (2024 focused update / 2026 anticipated):** Tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg is **COR 2b, LOE B-R** as a "reasonable alternative" to alteplase for eligible AIS patients per the 2019 AHA/ASA Guidelines for Early Management and the 2024 ESO/AHA harmonization. ORIGINAL strengthens but does not change the class of recommendation. **Verify against current AHA/ASA 2026 guidance before publishing the class label** — this packet's confidence is High for trial data, Medium for guideline class (guideline document not opened in this packet).
