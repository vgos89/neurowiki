## 1. SELECT-2
- Sarraj A, NEJM 2023 Apr 6;388(14):1259–1271
- DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2214403 · PMID: 36762865 · NCT03876457
- Population: anterior LVO, within 24h LKW, ASPECTS 3–5 (NCCT) or core ≥50 mL (CTP/DWI), prestroke mRS 0–1, n=352
- Primary endpoint: ordinal mRS at 90 days
- Statistical design: ordinal-shift (generalized OR)
- Primary result: **gOR 1.51 (95% CI 1.20–1.89), P<0.001** favoring EVT; mRS 0–2: 20% vs 7%; sICH 0.6% vs 1.1% (NS)
- AHA/ASA 2026: **Class 1, LOE A** (§4.7.2 Rec 3) for ASPECTS 3–5
- Quoted text: "Among patients with large ischemic strokes, endovascular thrombectomy resulted in better functional outcomes than medical care but was associated with vascular complications and intracranial hemorrhages."
- NNT: not appropriate (ordinal-shift primary)
- Caveats: companion trial to ANGEL-ASPECT (NEJM 2023 joint editorial)

## 2. ANGEL-ASPECT
- Huo X, NEJM 2023 Apr 6;388(14):1272–1283
- DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2213379 · PMID: 36762852 · NCT04551664
- Population: Chinese adults, anterior LVO within 24h, ASPECTS 3–5 OR core 70–100 mL, n=455
- Primary endpoint: shift in mRS at 90 days
- Statistical design: ordinal-shift (generalized OR)
- Primary result: **gOR 1.37 (95% CI 1.11–1.69), P=0.004** favoring EVT; mRS 0–3: 47.0% vs 33.3%; sICH 6.1% vs 2.7% (P=0.07)
- AHA/ASA 2026: **Class 1, LOE A** (§4.7.2 Rec 3)
- Quoted text: "In a trial conducted in China, patients with large cerebral infarctions had better outcomes with endovascular therapy administered within 24 hours than with medical management alone but had more intracranial hemorrhages."
- NNT: not appropriate (ordinal-shift)
- Caveats: China-only enrollment; stopped early at interim for efficacy

## 3. LASTE
- Costalat V, NEJM 2024 May 9;390(18):1677–1689
- DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2314063 · PMID: 38718358 · NCT03811769
- Population: anterior LVO ≤6.5h, ASPECTS ≤5 with no lower bound, France+Spain, n=333
- Primary endpoint: mRS at 90 days
- Statistical design: ordinal-shift (generalized OR)
- Primary result: **gOR 1.63 (95% CI 1.29–2.06)** favoring EVT; median mRS 4 vs 6; all-cause death 36.1% vs 55.5% (RR 0.65); sICH 9.6% vs 5.7%
- AHA/ASA 2026: **Class 2a, LOE B-R** (§4.7.2 Rec 4) for ASPECTS 0–2
- Quoted text: "In patients with acute stroke and a large infarct of unrestricted size, thrombectomy plus medical care resulted in better functional outcomes and lower mortality than medical care alone but led to a higher incidence of symptomatic intracerebral hemorrhage."
- NNT: not appropriate; trial-reported "NNT=4" is shift-derived only
- Caveats: stopped early Feb 2023

## 4. TENSION
- Bendszus M, Lancet 2023 Nov 11;402(10414):1753–1763
- DOI: 10.1016/S0140-6736(23)02032-9 · PMID: 37837989 · NCT03094715
- Population: anterior LVO, ASPECTS 3–5 by NCCT only (no advanced imaging), up to 12h, EU + Canada, n=253
- Primary endpoint: mRS shift at 90 days
- Statistical design: ordinal-shift (adjusted common OR)
- Primary result: **adjusted cOR 2.58 (95% CI 1.60–4.15), P=0.0001** favoring EVT; mortality 40% vs 51% (P=0.038); sICH 5% vs 5% (NS — NCT selection did not increase hemorrhage)
- AHA/ASA 2026: contributes to **Class 1, LOE A** (§4.7.2 Rec 3); de-emphasizes mandatory CTP for large-core decision
- Quoted text: "Endovascular thrombectomy was associated with improved functional outcome and lower mortality in patients with acute ischaemic stroke from large vessel occlusion with established large infarct in a setting using non-contrast CT as the predominant imaging modality for patient selection."
- NNT: not appropriate (ordinal-shift)
- Caveats: stopped early at first interim

## 5. OPTIMAL-BP
- Nam HS, JAMA 2023 Sep 5;330(9):832–842
- DOI: 10.1001/jama.2023.14590 · PMID: 37668619 · NCT04205305
- Population: South Korea, anterior LVO post-EVT with mTICI ≥2b, n=306
- Intervention: SBP <140 (intensive) vs 140–180 (conventional) × 24h post-reperfusion
- Primary endpoint: functional independence (mRS 0–2) at 90d
- Statistical design: superiority (binary primary)
- Primary result: **mRS 0–2: 39.4% (intensive) vs 54.4% (conventional); aOR 0.56 (95% CI 0.33–0.96), P=0.03** — intensive lowering HARMFUL; risk difference −15.1%
- AHA/ASA 2026: contributes to §4.3 "intensive lowering not recommended post-EVT"
- Quoted text: "These results suggest that intensive BP management should be avoided after successful EVT in acute ischemic stroke."
- NNT/NNH: **NNH ≈ 7** for loss of functional independence at SBP <140 — display as NNH, never NNT
- Caveats: stopped early for harm signal; truncation-bias caveat

## 6. ENCHANTED2/MT
- Yang P, Lancet 2022 Nov 5;400(10363):1585–1596
- DOI: 10.1016/S0140-6736(22)01882-7 · PMID: 36341753 · NCT04140110
- Population: Chinese adults post-EVT with successful reperfusion, persistent SBP ≥140 mmHg, n=821
- Intervention: SBP <120 (more intensive) vs 140–180 (less intensive) for 72h
- Primary endpoint: mRS shift at 90 days
- Statistical design: ordinal-shift (common OR)
- Primary result: **cOR 1.37 (95% CI 1.07–1.76) for POOR outcome favoring less-intensive arm** — i.e., SBP <120 was HARMFUL; major disability OR 2.07 (95% CI 1.47–2.93)
- AHA/ASA 2026: foundation for "avoid SBP <120 post-EVT" in §4.3
- Quoted text: "Intensive control of systolic blood pressure to lower than 120 mm Hg should be avoided to prevent compromising the functional recovery of patients who have received endovascular thrombectomy for acute ischaemic stroke due to intracranial large-vessel occlusion."
- NNT: not appropriate (ordinal-shift, harm direction)
- Caveats: stopped early; China-only; <120 target more extreme than OPTIMAL-BP <140 — do not conflate

## 7. TRACE-III
- Xiong Y, NEJM 2024 Jul 18;391(3):203–212
- DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2402980 · PMID: 38884324 · NCT05141305
- Population: Chinese adults, anterior LVO, 4.5–24h LKW, salvageable tissue (CTP/MRP), NO ACCESS TO EVT, n=516
- Intervention: IV tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg vs standard medical
- Primary endpoint: mRS 0–1 at 90 days (binary)
- Statistical design: superiority (binary primary, dichotomized at mRS 0–1)
- Primary result: **mRS 0–1: 33.0% (TNK) vs 24.2% (control); RR 1.37 (95% CI 1.04–1.81), P=0.03**; sICH 3.0% vs 0.8%
- AHA/ASA 2026: **Class 2b, LOE B-R** (§4.6.3 Rec 3) for LVO patients who *cannot* receive EVT in extended window
- Quoted text: "In this trial involving Chinese patients with ischemic stroke due to large-vessel occlusion, most of whom did not undergo endovascular thrombectomy, treatment with tenecteplase administered within 4.5 to 24 hours after stroke onset resulted in less disability and similar survival as compared with standard medical treatment, and the incidence of symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage appeared to be higher."
- NNT: **APPROPRIATE** here — NNT ≈ 11 (1/[0.330−0.242]); pre-specified binary primary in superiority design. Display ARD 8.8% (CI 1.8–15.8%) alongside NNT.
- Caveats: China-only; "no access to EVT" does not translate to US/EU systems; stopped early for efficacy

## 8. TIMELESS
- Albers GW, NEJM 2024 Feb 22;390(8):701–711
- DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2310392 · PMID: 38329148 · NCT03785678
- Population: US/Canada, anterior LVO, 4.5–24h, salvageable tissue, NIHSS ≥5, n=458; **77% received EVT** (TNK effectively a bridging agent)
- Intervention: IV tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg vs placebo
- Primary endpoint: ordinal mRS at 90 days
- Statistical design: ordinal-shift (adjusted common OR)
- Primary result: **adjusted cOR 1.13 (95% CI 0.82–1.57), P=0.45** — no benefit; sICH ~3% both arms (NS)
- AHA/ASA 2026: **negative/limiting trial** — TIMELESS constrains TRACE-III's Class 2b to "cannot receive EVT" populations. Label COR `—` or `3:NB`, NOT 2b.
- Quoted text: "Tenecteplase therapy that was initiated 4.5 to 24 hours after stroke onset in patients with occlusions of the middle cerebral artery or internal carotid artery, most of whom had undergone endovascular thrombectomy, did not result in better clinical outcomes than those with placebo."
- NNT: not appropriate (negative trial, ordinal primary)
- Caveats: 77% received EVT (effectively bridging); M1 subgroup signal is post-hoc

## 9. BAOCHE
- Jovin TG, NEJM 2022 Oct 13;387(15):1373–1384
- DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2207576 · PMID: 36239645 · NCT02737189
- Population: Chinese adults, basilar artery occlusion, 6–24h, PC-ASPECTS ≥6, NIHSS ≥10 (amended), n=217 (2:1 EVT:medical)
- Primary endpoint: mRS 0–3 at 90 days (binary; amended from 0–4 during trial)
- Statistical design: superiority (binary primary)
- Primary result: **mRS 0–3: 46% (EVT) vs 24% (medical); aRR 1.81 (95% CI 1.26–2.60), P<0.001**; sICH 6% vs 1%
- AHA/ASA 2026: **Class 1, LOE A** for basilar AO, NIHSS ≥10, PC-ASPECTS ≥6, within 24h (§4.7.3 Rec 1)
- Quoted text (MEDIUM confidence — reconstructed): "Among patients with basilar-artery occlusion, thrombectomy performed between 6 and 24 hours after symptom onset resulted in a higher percentage of patients with good functional status at 90 days than best medical care but was associated with a higher incidence of procedural complications and symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage."
- NNT: appropriate per pre-specified binary primary; NNH for sICH ≈ 20
- Caveats: protocol amendment (primary outcome changed); NIHSS 6–9 stratum n=17 only

## 10. ATTENTION
- Tao C, NEJM 2022 Oct 13;387(15):1361–1372
- DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2206317 · PMID: 36239644 · NCT04751708
- Population: Chinese adults, basilar AO, ≤12h, NIHSS ≥10, n=340 (2:1 EVT:medical)
- Primary endpoint: mRS 0–3 at 90 days
- Statistical design: superiority (binary primary)
- Primary result: **mRS 0–3: 46% (EVT) vs 23% (medical); aRR 2.06 (95% CI 1.46–2.91), P<0.001**; mortality 37% vs 55%; sICH 5% vs 0%
- AHA/ASA 2026: **Class 1, LOE A** (§4.7.3 Rec 1)
- Quoted text (MEDIUM confidence — reconstructed): "Among Chinese patients with acute ischemic stroke due to basilar-artery occlusion, the addition of endovascular thrombectomy to best medical care within 12 hours after symptom onset resulted in better functional outcomes at 90 days than best medical care alone but was associated with procedural complications and intracerebral hemorrhage."
- NNT: appropriate per binary primary
- Caveats: stopped early; China-only

## 11. AcT
- Menon BK, Lancet 2022 Jul 16;400(10347):161–169
- DOI: 10.1016/S0140-6736(22)01054-6 · PMID: 35779553 · NCT03889249
- Population: Canadian adults, AIS within 4.5h, pragmatic enrollment (no special imaging), n=1577 ITT
- Intervention: TNK 0.25 mg/kg vs alteplase 0.9 mg/kg
- Primary endpoint: mRS 0–1 at 90–120 days
- Statistical design: **noninferiority** (NI margin Δ = −5% on lower 95% CI of unadjusted risk difference)
- Primary result: **mRS 0–1: 36.9% (TNK) vs 34.8% (alteplase); RD 2.1% (95% CI −2.6 to 6.9)** — NI met (lower CI −2.6% above margin −5%); sICH 3.4% vs 3.2% (NS); mortality 15.3% vs 15.4% (NS)
- AHA/ASA 2026: **Class 1, LOE A** (§4.6.2 Rec 1) — TNK equivalent to alteplase
- Quoted text: "Intravenous tenecteplase (0.25 mg/kg) is a reasonable alternative to alteplase for all patients presenting with acute ischaemic stroke who meet standard criteria for thrombolysis."
- NNT: **NOT APPROPRIATE** (NI design, CI crosses zero) — display NI margin chart only
- Caveats: pragmatic, no advanced imaging selection
