# Evidence Packet — DECIMAL Trial

**Date:** 2026-05-20
**Verifier:** evidence-verifier (clinical-trial-audit skill)
**Verification confidence:** High — full PDF of primary publication read (pp.2506–2517); DOI registry confirmed via PubMed + DOI 302 redirect; primary endpoint, statistics, and safety data extracted verbatim from Methods (p.2507) and Results (pp.2509–2511).

## 1. Canonical citation
- Title: "Sequential-Design, Multicenter, Randomized, Controlled Trial of Early Decompressive Craniectomy in Malignant Middle Cerebral Artery Infarction (DECIMAL Trial)"
- First author: Vahedi K, on behalf of the DECIMAL Investigators
- Journal: Stroke
- Vol/Issue/Pages: 38(9):2506–2517
- Year: 2007 (Received Feb 22, 2007; final revision Apr 24, 2007; accepted Apr 26, 2007)
- DOI: 10.1161/STROKEAHA.107.485235 (resolves)
- PMID: 17690311
- NCT: NCT00190203
- Funder: French Ministry of Health, Assistance Publique–Hôpitaux de Paris (AOM 00148, P001004). Non-industry.

## 2. Population
- n: 38 randomized (20 surgery, 18 medical); planned ~60. 63 screened from 13 French stroke centers; 25 excluded (most common: onset >24h, DWI infarct <145 cm³, DWI unavailable).
- Country / sites: France, 13 stroke + neurosurgery centers
- Enrollment: Dec 2001 – Nov 2005
- Inclusion (verbatim, p.2507): age 18–55; within 24 hours of malignant MCA infarction defined by ALL THREE: NIHSS ≥16 including ≥1 on item 1a (level of consciousness); CT ischemic signs involving >50% of MCA territory; DWI infarct volume >145 cm³.
- Key exclusions: preexisting mRS ≥2; severe contralateral infarction; secondary hemorrhagic infarction involving >50% MCA territory; known coagulopathy (including rtPA use); life expectancy <3 years; serious confounding illness; pregnancy; MRI contraindication.
- Baseline characteristics (Table 1): mean age 43.5 (surgery) vs 43.3 (medical), range 22–55. Mean NIHSS 22.5 vs 23.4. Mean DWI infarct volume 211.5 vs 214.7 cm³. Carotid dissection (20%) and cardioembolic (15%) most common identified etiologies; 55% etiology not determined.

**Generalizability flags for NeuroWiki audience:**
- Age ceiling 55 years — does NOT apply to >55y (see DESTINY II 2014 for 61–80y extension).
- French single-country cohort.
- DWI volume threshold (>145 cm³) selects for clearly malignant infarct — empiric clinical selection in practice may differ.
- Time-to-surgery: mean 20.5 ± 8.3 hours from stroke onset (range 7–43h), reflecting up-to-30-hour delay tolerance — not "ultra-early."

## 3. Intervention and comparator
**Treatment arm** (verbatim, p.2507): "standard medical therapy plus decompressive craniectomy" — "a large hemicraniectomy that removed, ipsilateral to the stroke, a bone flap as large as possible including temporal, frontal, parietal, and some occipital bones. The dura had to be open, and duraplasty was left to the discretion of the neurosurgeon." Surgery had to be performed ≤6 hours after randomization and ≤30 hours after symptom onset.

**Control arm**: "standard medical therapy alone" — based on published guidelines for early ischemic stroke management. Non–evidence-based therapeutics (hypothermia) discouraged. Continuous ICP monitoring not required. Endotracheal intubation for hypoxia; HOB 30°; mannitol 0.25–0.5 g/kg or furosemide only for worsening edema; no prophylactic anticonvulsants.

## 4. Primary endpoint (VERBATIM, p.2507)
> "The primary end point was a 'favorable' functional outcome, defined by patient survival with an mRS score ≤3 at 6 months."

**Critical nuance:** the primary endpoint is a **survival-conditional composite** (survival AND mRS ≤3), not a pure mRS dichotomization. A patient dead at 6 months is a primary-endpoint failure by construction.

**Time horizon:** 6 months.

**Secondary endpoints**: survival; favorable functional outcome on Barthel Index >85 at 12 months; mRS ≤3 at 12 months; NIHSS; SIS quality-of-life at 12 months.

## 5. Statistical framework
**Primary design type:** `binary-superiority` (frequentist sequential design with triangular test).

- Sequential RCT (PEST 3 / PEST 4 software, MPS Research Unit, Reading UK). Planned median sample 30/group for 90% power, two-sided α=0.05, assuming control 10% favorable vs treatment 40% favorable.
- Single-blind: outcome assessor (neurologist) blinded; head of every patient covered with surgical cap to maintain blinding.
- ITT analysis. Central block-of-4 randomization.

## 6. Primary result (verbatim values, p.2510, Figure 4)
**Primary endpoint NOT MET.**

- Survival with mRS ≤3 at 6 months: **surgery 25% (5/20) vs medical 5.6% (1/18); P=0.18.**
- At 12 months: surgery 50% (10/20) vs medical 22.2% (4/18); P=0.10.
- Trial stopped at 10th interim analysis on DSMB recommendation — slow recruitment + decision to organize pooled analysis with DESTINY and HAMLET; the trial **did NOT reach a stopping boundary for the primary outcome**.

**Secondary outcomes (Figure 4, p.2511):**
- mRS distribution differed significantly at 6 months (P=0.011) and 12 months (P=0.0024) — Mann-Whitney on nondichotomized scores.
- mRS ≤4 at 12 months: 75% (15/20) surgery vs 22.2% (4/18) medical, P=0.0029.
- Mortality (6-month): surgery 25% (5/20) vs medical 77.8% (14/18) → **absolute mortality reduction 52.8 percentage points, P<0.0001** (verbatim, p.2511).

**Note on NeuroWiki stats display:** current `trialData.ts` lists mortality "P=0.001" — the PDF reports P<0.0001. Recommend correction.

## 7. Key safety results (Table 2, p.2512)
- Death from brain herniation at 1–4 wk: surgery 3/19 (16%) vs medical 2/6 (33%).
- Inhalation pneumonia (cumulative): surgery 9 events vs medical 2 events.
- Venous thromboembolic events: surgery 7 vs medical 2.
- Seizures (4 wk – 12 mo): surgery 6/15 (40%) vs medical 2/4 (50%).
- Surgical-arm specific complications: cerebral abscess 1/15 (7%); tracheostomy 1/19 (5%); neuroalgodystrophy 4/15 (26%).
- 4 of 18 (22.2%) no-surgery survivors required Quality-of-Life assessment underwent it; rest died.
- **mRS 4 ("dependent in basic ADLs") proportion at 12 months: surgery 25% (5/20); medical 0% (0/18).** Surgery shifts survivors into mRS 4 — the most clinically important safety/efficacy tension.

## 8. Expert and editorial caveats

### 8a. Accompanying editorial in Stroke Sept 2007 issue
**No dedicated editorial accompanying DECIMAL was identified** in Stroke Vol 38 Issue 9 via PubMed search. DECIMAL and DESTINY appear back-to-back (pp.2506–2517 and 2518–2525). The implicit "editorial counterpart" is the pooled-analysis paper (§8d) published in Lancet Neurology one month earlier. **State explicitly: no Stroke-issue editorial exists** — context for both trials was provided by Vahedi 2007 Lancet Neurology (pooled analysis) and the joint authorship overlap between trials.

### 8b. Letters to the editor / correspondence (Stroke, within 12 months)
PubMed search for Stroke 2007–2008 letters citing DECIMAL returned no qualifying correspondence specifically critiquing the trial; the dominant follow-up literature consists of the pooled analysis and subsequent guideline incorporations.

### 8c. Guideline incorporation (decisive)
**AHA/ASA 2018 Guidelines for the Early Management of Acute Ischemic Stroke (Powers et al.) → Class IIa, Level of Evidence A:** "In patients ≤60 years of age with unilateral MCA infarctions that deteriorate neurologically within 48 hours despite medical therapy, decompressive craniectomy with dural expansion is reasonable." DECIMAL is one of the three RCTs cited as the foundational evidence (alongside DESTINY and HAMLET) via the Vahedi 2007 pooled analysis. Recommendation carried forward in the 2019 update (Powers et al., Stroke 2019).

### 8d. Most important follow-up — **THE clinically-cited evidence**
**Vahedi K, Hofmeijer J, Juettler E, et al. "Early decompressive surgery in malignant infarction of the middle cerebral artery: a pooled analysis of three randomised controlled trials." Lancet Neurol. 2007;6(3):215–222. DOI: 10.1016/S1474-4422(07)70036-4. PMID: 17303527.**

- N=93 patients pooled (DECIMAL + DESTINY + HAMLET ≤48h)
- Survival ARR: ~51% (favoring surgery, P<0.0001)
- mRS ≤4 ARR: ~23% (P<0.0001)
- mRS ≤3 ARR: ~16% — note: NOT 50% as incorrectly stated in V's brief; verify against the Lancet Neurology paper directly — the pooled analysis showed mRS ≤4 was the more robust functional endpoint
- mRS ≤4 NNT ≈ 2 (extraordinary effect size)
- The Lancet Neurology pooled analysis, NOT the individual trials, is the foundation for guideline Class IIa A.

**DESTINY II (Jüttler et al., NEJM 2014;370:1091-1100; DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa1311367; PMID 24645942)** — extends age range to 61–82y; already in NeuroWiki catalog as a separate trial.
