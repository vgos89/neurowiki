- **Title:** Long-Term Outcomes of Patent Foramen Ovale Closure or Medical Therapy after Stroke
- **Journal:** N Engl J Med 2017;377(11):1022–1032
- **DOI:** 10.1056/NEJMoa1610057
- **PMID:** 28902590
- **ClinicalTrials.gov:** NCT00465270
- **Published:** September 14, 2017

## Trial identity

- Extended follow-up of the original RESPECT trial (Carroll JD et al., NEJM 2013;368:1092–1100, DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa1301440, PMID 23514285) reported here as the long-term outcome paper.
- Multicenter, randomized, open-label, 1:1, blinded endpoint adjudication.
- 69 sites in the US and Canada.
- Enrollment August 2003 to December 2011.
- **Median follow-up 5.9 years** (vs 2.1 years in the original 2013 report).
- Sponsored by St. Jude Medical. Used the Amplatzer PFO Occluder exclusively.

## Population

- Patients 18–60 years, cryptogenic ischemic stroke within 270 days, TEE-confirmed PFO.
- Excluded: identified mechanism (large-vessel, cardioembolic, small-vessel, hypercoagulable, dissection).
- **No atrial septal aneurysm or shunt-size requirement** — broader inclusion than CLOSE.
- 48.8% had a substantial (grade 3) right-to-left shunt; 35.7% had an atrial septal aneurysm.
- Original 2013 trial: 980 patients enrolled (499 PFO closure, 481 medical therapy).
- Mean age 45.9 years.

## Randomization and intervention

- 1:1 PFO closure vs medical therapy.
- Medical therapy: aspirin, warfarin, clopidogrel, or aspirin + extended-release dipyridamole (and initially aspirin + clopidogrel, removed in 2006 per practice change). **Medical therapy was clinician-chosen — included anticoagulation.** This is a key design difference from CLOSE and REDUCE.
- PFO closure performed within 21 days post-randomization; aspirin 81–325 mg + clopidogrel 75 mg daily for 1 month, then aspirin alone for 5 months, then per investigator discretion.

## Primary outcome and result

- **Primary efficacy endpoint:** composite of recurrent nonfatal ischemic stroke, fatal ischemic stroke, or early death after randomization (intention-to-treat).
- **Long-term result (this paper):**
  - Recurrent ischemic stroke: 18/499 (3.6%) PFO closure vs 28/481 (5.8%) medical therapy
  - Event rates: 0.58 vs 1.07 per 100 patient-years
  - **HR 0.55 (95% CI 0.31–0.999), P = 0.046 by log-rank**
  - Authors-reported NNT to prevent 1 recurrent stroke over 5 years: **42** (Discussion p.1030)
- **Recurrent ischemic stroke of undetermined cause (ASCOD):** 10/499 (2.0%) vs 23/481 (4.8%), HR 0.38 (0.18–0.79), P = 0.007.
- **Recurrent cryptogenic ischemic stroke (TOAST):** 1/499 (0.2%) vs 11/481 (2.3%), HR 0.08 (0.01–0.58), P = 0.01.

## Safety

- Serious adverse events: 40.3% PFO closure vs 36.0% medical therapy (P = 0.17). Mostly non-procedural over 5.9-year horizon.
- 25 device- or procedure-related serious adverse events in the PFO closure group (4.2% of 499): atrial fibrillation/flutter 7 events (1.4% of closure cohort, all resolved before discharge), cardiac perforation/thrombus/tamponade, pulmonary embolism, device-related ischemic stroke (2/499), residual shunt requiring closure (2/499).
- **Venous thromboembolism (DVT + PE) was higher in the PFO closure group:** PE 0.41 vs 0.11 per 100 patient-years (HR 3.48, 95% CI 0.98–12.34, P = 0.04); DVT 0.16 vs 0.04 per 100 patient-years (HR 4.44, 95% CI 0.52–38.05, P = 0.14). The medical-therapy arm received more intensive antithrombotic therapy (21.6% vs 3.3% patient-years on anticoagulation), which likely partly explains the VTE differential.
- **No procedure-related deaths.**

## Statistical design

- **primaryDesign: binary-superiority** (time-to-event composite, log-rank).
- **primaryResult: met** (HR 0.55, P = 0.046).
- The long-term analysis was pre-specified as exploratory at the extended-follow-up database lock (May 31, 2016); no alpha-spending adjustment was applied to the long-term P-value. Authors note that under a group-sequential Lan–DeMets framework the threshold would have been 0.043 — the result (P = 0.046) is essentially at this border. **Interpret the borderline P with appropriate caution.**
- NNT 42 is interpretable here (binary superiority over composite time-to-event endpoint with a single dichotomization).
- Differential dropout (33.3% medical vs 20.8% closure) is a known limitation; authors performed multiple imputation sensitivity analyses (HR 0.50, 95% CI 0.28–0.87, P = 0.02 after covariate adjustment) — direction and magnitude were robust.

## Subgroup heterogeneity (exploratory)

- Greater benefit in patients with **atrial septal aneurysm** (HR 0.20 vs 0.86, P interaction = 0.04).
- Greater benefit with **substantial shunt** (HR 0.26 vs 0.96, P interaction = 0.04).
- Suggestion of greater benefit when planned medical regimen was antiplatelet (HR 0.38) vs anticoagulation (HR 1.32, P interaction = 0.07).
- These subgroup findings align with CLOSE's restricted inclusion criteria.

## §8 editorial context

### §8a Accompanying NEJM editorial
- Ropper AH. Tipping Point for Patent Foramen Ovale Closure. N Engl J Med 2017;377(11):1093–1095.
- For RESPECT specifically: Ropper noted that the 2013 RESPECT primary did not reach significance in intention-to-treat, but the 5.9-year extension shifted the result. The extended follow-up converted a borderline 2013 result into a significant long-term benefit, with the strongest signal in undetermined-cause and cryptogenic-only recurrent strokes — supporting the mechanistic case for paradoxical embolism through PFO.

### §8b Post-publication NEJM letters
- Methodological critiques shared across the 2017 cluster (see CLOSE packet §8b for the full set). For RESPECT specifically: (1) differential dropout (33% medical vs 21% closure) raises concern about informative censoring; (2) the medical-therapy arm received substantial anticoagulation (21.6% vs 3.3% patient-years) — the comparator may have been a mixture of antiplatelet + warfarin rather than a clean antiplatelet comparator as in CLOSE/REDUCE; (3) borderline P = 0.046 against a group-sequential threshold of 0.043 limits the strength of the long-term conclusion taken in isolation.

### §8c Subsequent guideline incorporation
- **AHA/ASA 2021 Secondary Prevention Guideline** (Kleindorfer DO et al., Stroke 2021;52:e364–e467): PFO closure is reasonable (Class IIa, Level B-R) in non-lacunar stroke at age <60 with PFO and no other apparent etiology. CLOSE, RESPECT, and REDUCE cited together.
- **FDA approval (October 28, 2016):** Amplatzer PFO Occluder approved specifically for recurrent ischemic stroke prevention in cryptogenic stroke patients 18–60 with PFO. The approval was based primarily on the RESPECT long-term data (this paper).
- **AAN 2020 Practice Advisory** (Messé SR et al., Neurology 2020;94:876–885): same conclusion.

### §8d Subsequent meta-analyses
- Mir H, Siemieniuk RAC, Ge L, et al. BMJ 2018;362:k2515. IPD meta-analysis of 6 RCTs including RESPECT long-term: pooled HR 0.36 (95% CI 0.17–0.79) for recurrent stroke with closure.
- Kent DM, Saver JL, Kasner SE, et al. **PASCAL classification** (JAMA 2021;326:2277–2286): integrated RoPE score + PFO morphology to stratify likelihood of PFO-attributable stroke. The RESPECT subgroup data (ASA, substantial shunt) anchored the development of the morphology component of PASCAL.
