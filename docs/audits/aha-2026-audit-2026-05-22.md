Top consequential BLOCKING findings:

1. **NIHSS calculator EMR text omits LVO/EVT routing context** despite computing RACE and showing it in the drawer — clinicians who paste the EMR summary lose the LVO inference. Low clinical risk (drawer text is visible), but bedside speed is compromised.
2. **ASPECTS interpretation for score 0–2 calls EVT "Class IIa"** while the structured mirror records ASPECTS 0–2 as **COR 2a, LOE B-R** with explicit age-<80 and no-mass-effect criteria. Mirror agrees with prose direction but the numeric class label in the calculator says "Class IIa" while LASTE-derived guidance is actually 2a, LOE B-R — they match in substance; the BLOCKING risk is the unflagged conditional ("Exceptional cases ... require individualized consultation") understates the 2026 expansion. Recommend rewording.
3. **Stroke Code DAPT/post-thrombolysis card states "no antithrombotics × 24h"** — correct in substance per §4.8 (IV aspirin within 90 minutes of IVT is harmful) but the 24h figure is not the guideline language. Guideline says antiplatelet within 24h of IVT is "uncertain" (COR 2b) — not a flat ban. Risk: residents may withhold DAPT past 24h on the assumption of a hard ban.
4. **Stroke Code "Treatment Windows" pearl** describes thrombectomy as 0–24h with imaging selection — accurate generally — but does not flag the 2026 §4.7.2 expansion to ASPECTS 3–5 (COR 1, LOE A within 6–24h) and ASPECTS 0–2 (COR 2a, LOE B-R, age <80 + 0–6h + no mass effect). The omission is material because residents using the pearl as a quick reference will miss the eligible large-core patient.
5. **Stroke Code post-IVT BP guidance** ("BP <180/105") agrees with the guideline (§4.3 afterIVT: COR 1, LOE B-R) but the 4.5h window pearl conflates standard-window IVT with the EXTEND extended-window data — the line "extended windows possible up to 9 hours using perfusion imaging (EXTEND trial)" is correct, but the omitted-by-the-pearl detail is that 4.5–9h is now COR 2a, LOE B-R (§4.6.3 extendedWindows row 2) — not just "possible".
6. **ELAN pathway EMR text caption** ends with "AHA/ASA 2026 §4.9 — COR 2a. Early oral anticoagulation is reasonable in carefully selected (eg, milder severity) patients with AIS and atrial fibrillation. Efficacy for early recurrence prevention not established." — that text is accurate against the guideline. However, the same paragraph also embeds the COR label inside the JSX heading slot, producing a 250-char "class label" instead of a class chip. UI bug. Clinical substance correct.
7. **Extended IVT Path C result string** says "TRACE-III, sICH 3.0% vs 0.8%" — accurate against TRACE-III. But the pathway's COR label for Path C-LVO is "Class 2b, LOE B-R" — matches §4.6.3 Rec 3. Substantive AGREE. The BLOCKING item is that the pathway also offers Path C in the wake-up subset (`onsetMode === 'wake-up'` line 129) — guideline 2026 §4.6.3 row 3 wording is "presenting within 4.5–24 hours from symptom onset or last known well" — wake-up usability of TRACE-III is by extrapolation, not by trial enrollment. Wake-up patients in TRACE-III were a minority subgroup, not the enrolled population.
8. **EXTEND IVT modal `EXTENDED_WINDOW_CHIPS`** lists 3–4.5h ECASS-3 exclusions (age >80, NIHSS >25, oral anticoag, DM+prior stroke, >1/3 MCA) — these are based on ECASS-3 historic exclusions, not on the 2026 guideline. AHA/ASA 2026 §4.6.1 Rec 7 explicitly removes age >80 as an exclusion (treats it as a relative factor, not a hard exclusion). Risk: clinicians using the modal will incorrectly disqualify eligible patients.
9. **NIHSS drawer "RACE ≥5 has 85% sensitivity for LVO"** — Pérez de la Ossa 2014 figure. Not contradicted by AHA/ASA 2026. The BLOCKING surface is the *absence* of an §4.7 EVT cross-reference. The NIHSS drawer infers LVO probability but does not link to EVT eligibility — and §4.7.2 is the operational target. Recommend adding a one-line "If LVO: assess EVT eligibility per §4.7" footer.

## Methodology

Cross-reference performed by reading each in-scope file, extracting clinical claims (dosing, thresholds, time windows, COR/LOE labels, contraindications), and matching against the structured AHA/ASA 2026 data in `src/data/aha2026StrokeGuideline.ts` (validated mirror of source PDF). Direct PDF page reads were not required because the mirror is comprehensive for §2–§6 with verbatim COR/LOE/recommendation text. Sections audited:

- §2 prehospital / MSU / direct-EVT-transport
- §3 imaging
- §4.3 BP management (pre-IVT, post-IVT, pre-EVT, post-EVT, general)
- §4.4 temperature
- §4.5 glucose
- §4.6.1 IVT decision-making
- §4.6.2 agent choice (alteplase vs TNK)
- §4.6.3 extended windows (WAKE-UP / EXTEND / TRACE-III / TIMELESS)
- §4.6.4 desmoteplase / streptokinase / sonothrombolysis
- §4.6.5 special circumstances (DOAC, SCD, CRAO, pediatric)
- §4.7.1 EVT adults (0–6h ASPECTS 3–10; 6–24h ASPECTS ≥6 and 3–5; 0–6h ASPECTS 0–2; mRS 2 / mRS 3–4; M2)
- §4.7.2 EVT concomitant with IVT
- §4.7.3 posterior circulation (basilar AO)
- §4.7.5 EVT pediatric
- §4.8 antiplatelet (general, DAPT for minor AIS, in setting of IVT)
- §4.9 anticoagulation
- §4.10–4.11 adjunctive treatments not recommended
- §5 in-hospital management (stroke unit, dysphagia, DVT, depression, oxygen, early mobilization)
- §5 secondary prevention early (statins, BP lowering, AF anticoag)
- §5 cardiac monitoring
- §6 brain swelling, cerebellar infarction, seizures, hemorrhagic transformation
- §6 orolingual angioedema

Out-of-AIS surfaces (HAS-BLED, CHA₂DS₂-VASc, Boston Criteria, Heidelberg) were not scored against AHA/ASA 2026 because they are governed by different sources (AF guideline, CAA criteria paper, HT classification paper, ICH guideline).

The 5 never-drift categories (recommendation strength, action verbs, qualifiers/gates, certainty markers, temporal constraints) were checked on every AGREE → MINOR DRIFT → BLOCKING classification.
