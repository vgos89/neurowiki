## Question-flow / branching ruling (safety) — RATIFIED, upgraded to hard conditions Q1–Q4
The author's ruling that conditional branching is REQUIRED for clinical safety is CORRECT. Independently confirmed against the engine: the five gated chip-groups (aura, vestibular, tac-detail, chronic-migraine-detail, indomethacin) are genuinely unreachable from a fixed 6-question spine, and the phenotypes depending on them (migraine-with-aura, vestibular migraine, paroxysmal hemicrania + hemicrania continua [`hiddenUntilTrial`], SUNCT, chronic migraine) cannot reach `full` (several cannot reach the array at all) without those chips. A flow that permanently caps cluster, the indomethacin-responsive TACs, and SUNCT at "Less likely" would systematically under-rank the phenotypes where early recognition most changes management — a patient-safety failure. "Less likely until a branch fires" is SAFE only because the branch is GUARANTEED to fire on substrate features (hence Q2). The proposed branch triggers are clinically correct (TAC short-attack detail on very-short + unilateral + autonomic; indomethacin on the unilateral+autonomic pattern; aura when migraine in contention; chronic-migraine on ≥15 days + migraine feature; vestibular on vertigo+headache). The SNNOOP10 `anyRedFlagActive` short-circuit correctly stays the gate before any banding.
- **Q1 (upgraded to REQUIRED).** Fold the attack-count chip (`attacks-5-to-10`/`-gt-10`/`-ge-2`) into the flow — without it migraine/cluster/TTH cap at `probable` (their A-criterion never fires). Verify a path exists for migraine-without-aura, episodic-tth, cluster-headache to reach `full`.
- **Q2 (safety linchpin).** Every branch trigger fires when its substrate features are in `selected`; triggers re-evaluate on answer change (bind architect Q1(a): revisiting an answer replaces that answer's chips and re-ranks, never wipes later answers). Tests for TAC/indomethacin, vestibular, chronic-migraine, aura branch firing.
- **Q3.** Resolve aura-branch reachability for aura-predominant presentations — fire on transient reversible neuro symptoms independent of a migraine band being present (preferred), OR document the "only once migraine in contention" gate as clinically acceptable. State in `headacheQuestions.ts` and test.
- **Q4.** All `[PAIR — preserve both]` mappings preserved (orbital → loc-unilateral + loc-orbital-temporal; continuous → dur-continuous). Bind architect Q2 drift-guard: every mapped chip resolves via `getChip(id)`, many-to-one preserved (single-id collapse is a clinical regression).
- **SNNOOP10 gate.** Whenever `anyRedFlagActive(selected)`, result leads with secondary-workup; no question runs ahead of the safety gate; the dangerous-mimic strip renders even in the empty/no-match case (bind architect Q7(2)).
