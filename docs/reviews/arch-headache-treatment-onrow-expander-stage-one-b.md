# Architect review — headache treatment on-row expander (Stage One-b)

**Decision:** approve-with-conditions
**Reviewed:** plan only
**Reviewer:** system-architect (model: claude-opus-4-8)
**Date:** 2026-06-05

## Rationale
The plan is structurally sound and continuous with the Stage One increment it builds on — it composes only, touches no `Pathway*` primitive, no engine, no state, no questionnaire, and leaves the `!redFlagActive` short-circuit intact. **On Q1 — the load-bearing call — lifting the hand-written per-phenotype JSX wholesale into a `phenotypeId`-keyed `HeadacheManagement` component is correct; do not data-restructure.** The current treatment block is ~20 registered `data-claim` dosing surfaces expressed as bespoke `Row label/value` pairs whose label keys are non-uniform across phenotypes ("High-flow O₂", "Week 1", "After 2 failures", "MOH limits") and whose grouping (1–3 cards per phenotype, with conditional gating like the `chronic-tth`-only preventive card at 757–766 and the ndph inline `Row` at 811) does not reduce to a clean record shape without lossy flattening. A data-driven management map would force transcribing 20 reviewed clinical strings into a new structure — exactly the transcription-error surface the "verbatim relocation is a hard requirement" constraint exists to forbid, and it would convert a clean Class-D structural move into a D-clinical/Class-E diff requiring full re-review. Keyed-component-with-verbatim-JSX keeps fidelity, keeps churn to a `switch (phenotypeId)` or a JSX-fragment map, and is the same fork pattern Stage One already chose; the only structural cost is that `HeadacheManagement` is bespoke to this one surface, which is acceptable and self-evident (no second consumer exists) and should be stated in a one-line header note rather than left silent. **On Q3 — a "Show management" `<details>` nested inside the accordion row's open body is a disclosure-within-a-disclosure, and that is acceptable here, not a structural smell:** the outer accordion is custom (button + `aria-expanded` + `hidden`, not native `<details>`), the inner control is a separate native disclosure scoped to a distinct concern (criteria vs. management), the nesting depth is exactly two, and the inner control is only rendered for `full`/`probable` rows so the common case stays shallow — keeping management *inside* the row preserves the spatial "this phenotype → its management" binding that a sibling-section-keyed-off-open-row design would break (and a sibling section keyed to open rows would also re-introduce open-state coupling that Stage One deliberately kept local). The two real risks are not in the component shape but in the relocation bookkeeping (Q2/Q6), pinned below. **Q2 is sound with one hazard:** moving `data-claim="clinic-headache-ichd3-<type>-criteria"` onto the row's existing `CriteriaList` render is the right home — the criteria claim renders once, tagged, where the criteria actually show — and because the accordion already renders `CriteriaList` for *every* match including partials, a partial-strength row will now legitimately carry the criteria claim; that is fine (the claim is the ICHD-3 criteria definition, which is true regardless of match strength) and is in fact more correct than today's top-match-only tagging, **provided each ID renders exactly once.** The `phenotypeId → criteriaClaimId` map must be **many-to-one**: `migraine-without-aura` + `migraine-with-aura` share `clinic-headache-ichd3-migraine-criteria`, and `episodic-tth` + `chronic-tth` share `clinic-headache-ichd3-tension-criteria` (verified at page 718–720 and 745–747; eight IDs across ten-ish phenotypes, all registered in `claims.ts` 778–899). Rubric-by-rubric: duplication (1) passes — no new pattern, helpers move with their sole consumer; boundary (2) passes — UI stays in components, claim IDs stay in the registry, this is a render-site move not a registry edit; composability (3) — `HeadacheManagement` is intentionally bespoke, name it; state locality (4) passes — the inner disclosure owns its own open state, nothing lifted, the `topMatch`-only→strong-matches change (Q4) is clean because `HeadacheResultList` already computes `significantMatches` and already iterates all rows, so rendering management on each `full`/`probable` row adds no new state and no new boundary; dependency weight (5) passes — no package; migration exit (6) passes — three files, under threshold, revert is restoring 716–866 plus the two helpers, no schema/shared-abstraction change, the prior ADR's rollback note covers the shape. Helper ownership (Q5): grep confirms `SectionHeader`/`Row` have zero consumers outside the 716–865 block and the two declarations at 972/978, and zero usage anywhere else under `src/components/pathways` — moving them into `HeadacheManagement` is right, not a shared module (one consumer = no shared module).

## Required follow-ups
- **Make the `phenotypeId → criteriaClaimId` map many-to-one and assert single-render per claim ID (rubric 2 — required).** Eight criteria claim IDs serve ten phenotype branches: `migraine-without-aura` and `migraine-with-aura` both map to `clinic-headache-ichd3-migraine-criteria`; `episodic-tth` and `chronic-tth` both map to `clinic-headache-ichd3-tension-criteria`. The map must reflect this, and the plan must confirm that across a full render each of the eight IDs appears on exactly one element — never zero (claim disappears → `check:claims` regression) and never two (e.g. if both migraine rows are present and both stamp the same ID, that is a duplicate-tag hazard). State the dedup rule explicitly: tag the criteria claim once per *claim ID* rendered, not once per phenotype row.
- **Confirm the migraine/tension criteria claim renders even when its row is collapsed (rubric 2 — required).** The accordion hides the row body with `hidden={!isOpen}` rather than unmounting it (HeadacheResultList line 136 renders `CriteriaList` inside a `hidden`-toggled region, not conditionally mounted), so the `data-claim` element stays in the DOM when collapsed — good, the claim does not vanish on collapse. Verify the new criteria-claim wrapper sits on the same always-mounted node (inside the `hidden` region, not gated on `isOpen`), so the scanner and any runtime claim check see it regardless of open state. If the implementer switches the row body to conditional mount, the claim disappears for collapsed rows — block that.
- **Account for every string in the deleted block — three relocation hazards by name (rubric 1 + Q6 — required).** (a) The **ndph** branch (807–813) is structurally different: its criteria claim card contains an inline `<Row label="Management" …>` *inside* the criteria `data-claim` div — so its management is NOT a separate dosing card. When the criteria render moves to the row and `HeadacheManagement` renders ndph's dosing, that "Management" Row must land in `HeadacheManagement` (ndph has no separate dosing card otherwise) and must not be orphaned in the criteria relocation. (b) The **`chronic-tth`-only preventive card** (757–766) is gated `topMatch.phenotypeId === 'chronic-tth'` *inside* the shared tension branch — `HeadacheManagement` keyed by `phenotypeId` must reproduce that gate so `episodic-tth` does not gain the chronic preventive card and `chronic-tth` does not lose it. (c) Every other dosing card's `data-claim` and `Row` text moves byte-for-byte. State in the PR that the moved literals were diffed against 716–865.
- **Name `HeadacheManagement` as intentionally bespoke (rubric 3 — required, one line).** Add a top-of-file note: this component is a single-surface home for the relocated clinic-headache dosing cards, not a general management renderer; if a second pathway needs per-phenotype management cards, revisit for a shared shape. This mirrors the Stage One `HeadacheResultList` fork note and keeps the bespoke choice tracked rather than silent.
- **State the exclusion explicitly: criteria card is NOT moved into `HeadacheManagement` (rubric 1 — required).** The plan says `HeadacheManagement` renders dosing only and excludes the ICHD-3 criteria card. Confirm the criteria card's `SectionHeader>ICHD-3 §… criteria</SectionHeader>` + `<CriteriaList>` wrapper is deleted from the page entirely (the criteria now render via the row's existing `CriteriaList`, with the claim moved onto it) — it is neither duplicated into `HeadacheManagement` nor left behind in the page. This is the duplication this whole increment removes; verify it is gone, not relocated twice.
- **Routing to clinical-reviewer — conditional, not required.** If every relocated dosing string, the ndph Management Row, and the criteria-claim placement are byte-identical to 716–865 (verifiable by diff of the moved literals), this stays Class D and needs no clinical gate. The one *behavioral* clinical change — management now shows for `full`/`probable` matches and for >1 row when ≥2 strong matches, instead of top-match-only-at-any-strength — is a clinical-display-policy change the plan already flags for clinical-reviewer; that threshold ("full|probable", and the multi-row management exposure) must be ratified by `clinical-reviewer` before execution even though no string changes, because *which* management a clinician sees and under what match confidence is a clinical-safety call (§4 tier 1), not a structural one. State in the PR that the threshold is clinical-reviewer-gated.

## Blocking issues
None. Three files touched, all boundaries intact, the red-flag safety path and engine untouched, revert trivial (restore 716–866 + the two helpers, delete one component file). The conditions convert the two real structural risks — the many-to-one criteria-claim map (where a naive 1:1 map silently drops or double-stamps a registered claim ID and breaks `check:claims`) and the three irregular relocations (ndph's inline Management Row, the `chronic-tth`-only preventive gate, the criteria-card deletion-not-duplication) — into checkable conditions, and correctly route the one clinical-policy change (management visibility threshold) to the clinical-reviewer gate rather than letting "no string changed" mask a change in what management a clinician is shown.
