Secondary headache — urgent workup required.

Thunderclap onset (peak severity within seconds) is a SNNOOP10 red flag that mandates exclusion of subarachnoid haemorrhage and other secondary causes before a primary headache diagnosis is considered. Phenotyping cannot proceed until secondary cause is excluded.

Recommended workup — Thunderclap headache
  1. 1. Non-contrast CT head Sensitivity ~98% for SAH within 6 h of onset. Obtain immediately.
  2. 2. Lumbar puncture (if CT negative) Obtain >2 h after onset. Xanthochromia (visual or spectrophotometric) or RBC count >2000/μL indicates SAH.
  3. 3. CTA brain (with or after CT/LP) Evaluate for intracranial aneurysm, RCVS, CVT, arterial dissection. Obtain if CT or LP positive, or if high clinical suspicion persists despite negative initial workup.
