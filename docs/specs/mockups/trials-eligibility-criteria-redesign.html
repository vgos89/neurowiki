Full inclusion criteria

General inclusion criteria

  • Age ≥18 years
  • Stroke onset 6–24 hours before randomisation (or last seen well)
  • NIHSS score ≥10 at time of randomisation
  • Pre-stroke mRS 0–1
  • Occlusion of ICA or MCA M1 confirmed on CTA/MRA
  • Clinical-imaging mismatch: volume of ischaemic core ≤51 mL with severity/age criteria
  • Informed consent obtained

Imaging inclusion criteria

  • Ischaemic core volume ≤51 mL on CTP or DWI
  • Mismatch ratio ≥1.8 (salvageable tissue to core) on perfusion imaging

Full exclusion criteria

General exclusion criteria

  • Intracranial haemorrhage on baseline imaging
  • Blood glucose <50 mg/dL or >400 mg/dL
  • Platelet count <50,000/µL
  • Severe uncontrolled hypertension (SBP >185 mmHg or DBP >110 mmHg)
  • Pregnancy or lactation
  • Life expectancy <3 months prior to stroke onset
  • Rapidly improving neurological status

Imaging exclusion criteria

  • Ischaemic core >51 mL on CTP or DWI
  • Isolated posterior circulation occlusion without mismatch
  • CT evidence of established large-territory infarct (ASPECTS ≤5)

Source: ClinicalTrials.gov NCT02142283 · Retrieved 2026-06-08

Toggle variant — owner picks one

Option A — Card-level toggle

not recommended
Show all criteria (both columns)

One button expands both columns simultaneously. Simpler interaction surface: one toggle, one action.

Trade-offs

+ Minimal interaction, easy to implement, single aria-expanded pair.

- Forces the clinician to see both columns expand even when they only care about one side (e.g., quickly checking exclusion criteria at the bedside).

- On narrow viewports (single-column), the columns stack — a card-level toggle must visually position itself sensibly between them, which is awkward.

Option B — Per-column toggle

recommended

Included

Show all 9 criteria

Excluded

Show all 10 criteria

Each column expands independently. The toggle sits at the foot of its column, directly below the list it controls.

Trade-offs

+ Bedside workflow: a clinician checking exclusion criteria only expands that column. No unnecessary scroll through inclusion criteria.

+ Spatially obvious — the toggle is visually inside the column it controls.

+ Stacks cleanly at single-column viewport widths (each column toggle is self-contained).

- Two toggle states to manage in state (minor; both are useState<boolean>).

Recommendation — Option B (per-column)

The per-column toggle aligns with the bedside use-case: a clinician scanning eligibility during a stroke code is typically checking one side at a time ("does this patient meet inclusion?", "any absolute exclusions?"). Expanding both columns simultaneously adds scroll load when only one side is needed. The implementation cost is trivial — two useState booleans, one per column — and the toggle placement (at the foot of each column, in-column) is spatially unambiguous. Option A would require awkward toggle placement at the card level that fights the two-column visual rhythm.
