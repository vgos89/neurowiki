Features consistent with Migraine without aura.

All 4 ICHD-3 criteria are met (≥5 attacks, 4–72 h, ≥2 headache features, ≥1 associated symptom). Lifetime attack count >10 confirms episodic migraine. No aura features reported.

ICHD-3 Criteria — Migraine without aura (1.1)
  • ✓ A. At least 5 attacks fulfilling criteria B–D Lifetime attacks >10 — criterion A met
  • ✓ B. Headache lasting 4–72 hours Duration per attack: 4–72 h — criterion B met
  • ✓ C. ≥2 of: unilateral, pulsating, moderate–severe, aggravated by activity All 4 character features met — criterion C met
  • ✓ D. ≥1 of: nausea/vomiting, photophobia and phonophobia Nausea + photophobia + phonophobia — criterion D met
Acute treatment
  • Triptans — first-line for moderate–severe attacks (sumatriptan 50–100 mg PO or 6 mg SC)
  • NSAIDs — naproxen 500–550 mg PO; ibuprofen 400–600 mg PO for mild–moderate
  • Gepants — rimegepant 75 mg, ubrogepant 50–100 mg; useful when triptans contraindicated
  • Antiemetics — prochlorperazine 10 mg PO/IV if nausea prominent
Preventive treatment

Threshold met — 5–14 headache days/month (AHS Level A preventive threshold ≥4 days/month)

  • CGRP mAbs — erenumab, fremanezumab, galcanezumab (first-line if >8 days/month or prior preventive failure)
  • Topiramate — 25–100 mg/day; Level A evidence; teratogenic — avoid in childbearing age
  • Propranolol — 40–240 mg/day; Level A evidence; avoid in asthma, bradycardia
  • Amitriptyline — 10–75 mg nightly; Level B evidence; useful with comorbid insomnia
