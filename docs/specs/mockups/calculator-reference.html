High-risk intracerebral hemorrhage.

An ICH Score of 3 corresponds to 72% 30-day mortality. This score reflects prognosis at admission and is not a decision rule for withdrawal of care. Outcomes improve with aggressive ICU management, BP control (SBP <140), and reversal of anticoagulation. Avoid early pessimism.

Moderate bleeding risk.

Approximately 3.74 major bleeds per 100 patient-years. Address modifiable factors and monitor. Do not withhold anticoagulation for stroke prevention based on HAS-BLED alone.

Important

HAS-BLED estimates bleeding risk. It does not mean "do not anticoagulate." Use it to address modifiable factors (BP, alcohol, NSAIDs, labile INR). Anticoagulation is driven by stroke risk (CHA₂DS₂-VASc) and shared decision-making.

See also

CHA₂DS₂-VASc·Anticoagulation in AF guidelines

Pisters R, Lane DA, Nieuwlaat R, et al. A novel user-friendly score (HAS-BLED) to assess 1-year risk of major bleeding in patients with atrial fibrillation. Chest. 2010;138(5):1093–1100. View source

Educational use only. High HAS-BLED does not contraindicate anticoagulation.
